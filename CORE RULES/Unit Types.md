### Vehicle Squadrons

Units consisting of multiple vehicles are called **SQUADRONS** and are subject to the following additional rules:

* **COHERENCY:** Coherency for vehicle squadrons is 6”.  
* **MOVEMENT:** Vehicles in a squadron are moved individually and may each be moved at different speeds, so long as coherency is maintained at the end of the unit’s movement.  
* **SHOOTING:** The speed an individual vehicle model in a squadron moves will determine what weapons it is able to fire.  Likewise, each vehicle may select targets to shoot at independently.  If any vehicle in the squadron attempts to split fire, only make a single leadership test (using Leadership 10\) and apply the results to the entire squadron.  
* **ATTACKING SQUADRONS WITH RANGED ATTACKS:** Each vehicle in a squadron is treated as if it were its own unit when determining line of sight and range to models shooting at it (or engaging it in melee).  
  * When a unit fires on a squadron with ranged attacks, the attacking player decides, before rolling to hit, how their unit’s shots will be allocated to vehicles in the squadron.   
  * Successful hits are only resolved against the specific vehicle model that the attack was allocated to.  
* **CREW STUNNED RESULTS:** Individual vehicles in a squadron treat all crew stunned damaged results as crew shaken so long as at least one other vehicle in the squadron can still move (i.e. it is not immobilized or stunned).  
* **ABANDONING VEHICLES:** Immobilized vehicles OR those suffering Engine Damage in a squadron may be left behind and are treated as a distinct unit for the remainder of the game. The immobilized model may continue to shoot at a viable target.

### Chariots

* Move as normal for a vehicle of their type, counting as an **OPEN-TOPPED**, unless stated otherwise.  
* Chariots are shot at as a normal vehicle, the rider cannot be targeted separately.  
* In assault, the rider is treated as in base-to-base contact with all enemy models touching the chariot.  
  * The Chariot and the rider are not locked in combat and may freely disengage.  
  * Units attacking the chariot may allocate attacks freely between the rider or the chariot, but must declare which before rolling to hit.  
  * The chariot rider gains \+1 to its armor save rolls in close combat.  
* Chariot rider gains Fearless  
* The rider may not disembark from the chariot unless the chariot is destroyed. If the rider is built into the chariot model and not able to be used as a standalone piece, then if either the rider or the chariot model are killed the entire unit is removed from play.

* **DEPLOYMENT**: All **FLYERS** must be placed in **RESERVES** at the start of the game.  
* **ENTERING PLAY**: When the FLYER unit is eligible to enter play, it moves on to the board from a table edge in their own deployment zone.    
* **MOVEMENT MODES:** Flyers move in **ZOOMING** mode unless the unit type also allows the **HOVER** type. For flyers with hover mode, the controlling player must specify at the start of their movement phase what movement mode the flyer unit will move in.  
  * When entering play from reserves, the controlling player must declare what movement mode the flyer is using before moving it onto the board.  
* **HOVER MODE**: Flyers moving in hover mode are treated exactly like **FAST SKIMMERS**.  
* **ZOOMING MODE**: Zooming Flyers must move at least 18” (Combat Speed). They may move up to 36” (Cruising Speed) or up to 60” (Flat Out Speed). Flyers always move in a straight line in the direction the model is facing.  
  * **TURNING**: Before moving, a flyer may pivot ONCE up to 90-degrees  
  * **FLYING OFF THE BOARD**: If the flyers movement would take it off the board, the model is removed from the table and enters **ONGOING RESERVES** (automatically re-enters play next turn from the controlling players table edge).  
* **SHOOTING AT ZOOMING FLYERS**  
  * **HARD TO HIT**: Shots against zooming flyers are resolved as follows:  
    * Combat speed (18”): Roll to hit normally  
    * Cruising speep”): Only hit on a 5+ (regardless of BS).    
    * Flat out speed (over 36”): Only hit with snap fire (6+).d (18-3  
  * **EVASION**: A targeted zooming Flyer may enter evasion mode before ranged attacks targeting it make their hit rolls. Evasion mode has the following effect:  
    * If moving at combat or cruising speed, the flyer gains  5+ cover save.  
    * If moving at flat out speed, the flyers gains a 4+ cover save.  
    * The flyer only shoots using snap fire in its next turn.  
  * **DAMAGE RESULTS**:   
    * **IMMOBILIZED:** Immobilized Zooming Flyers that had moved at Flat Out speed in their last movement phase are wrecked. If the zooming flyer move at combat or cruising speed, the vehicle can only move at combat speed for the rest of the game (in flyer or hover mode).  A second immobilized result will then wreck the flyer.  
    * Wrecked zooming flyers scatter 2D6” and are removed from play. Center the large blast markers where the center of the model and resolve hits on covered units as S6 AP- automatic hits.  Place a suitable wreckage model or crater on the location where the blast marker was placed if there is room to do so.  
    * PASSENGERS & WRECKED FLYERS: A unit embarked on a wrecked flyer takes an automatic hit that wounds on a 4+ for each model in the unit.  No armor saves may be taken.  Invulnerable saves may be used.

### Superheavy Vehicles

| OPTIONAL RULE\! Banning/Allowing Superheavy Vehicles *A play group may choose to ban Superheavy Vehicles altogether, as there are strong arguments to be made for their removal from 40K.  This ruleset is designed with the usage of a Superheavy Vehicles ban in mind, but rules for them are included for the sake of completeness.  Operate under the assumption that Superheavy Vehicles are not permitted for use unless your gaming group chooses to allow them.   Always ask your opponent before bringing Superheavy Vehicles to a game.* |
| :---- |

* **STRUCTURE POINTS**: All Superheavy Vehicles double the STRUCTURE POINTS granted to them by their Armor Values.  
* **VEHICLE TYPE**: Unless otherwise noted, the Superheavy Vehicle uses all the rules of its type (Tank, Walker, Flyer, Etc.)    
* **JINK**: Regardless of their type, Superheavy Vehicles may never use the Jink Special Rule under any circumstances.  
* **SHOOTING**: When a Super-heavy vehicle makes a shooting attack, it is always treated as if it had remained [stationar](https://scoolov.github.io/wh40000rules/the-rules/vehicles#TOC-Vehicles-in-the-Movement-Phase)y in the Movement phase (even if it actually moved), and it may fire each of its weapons at different targets without making a **SPLIT FIRE TEST**. In addition, firing Ordnance weapons has no effect on a Super-heavy vehicle’s ability to fire other weapons.  
* **GLANCING HITS**: A Superheavy Vehicle reduces all rolls on the Glancing Hit table by 1\.  Additionally, it reduces any roll of 7+ to a 6 on the Glancing Hit table, it does not further reduce the roll by 1 if the result is 7+.  
* **EXPLODES\!**: If an EXPLODES\! result is rolled on the Penetrating Hit table for a Superheavy Vehicle, remove the vehicle and replace it with a similarly-sized crater, which provides hard cover for models within the crater.  Additionally, all models within 3” of the vehicle's hull take an automatic Strength 10 AP 2 hit, all models within 6” of the vehicle’s hull take an an automatic Strength 6 AP 3 hit, and all models within 3D6” (minimum 7”) take a Strength 4 AP 5 hit.   Roll to wound and take saving throws as normal (cover saves may be taken).  
  * **EMBARKED PASSENGERS:** Automatically **PINNED** (even if normally immune to pinning). Additionally, each embarked unit takes a number of Strength 10 AP2 hits equal to the number of models in that unit. These Wounds are Randomly Allocated. Surviving passengers are placed where the vehicle used to be; any models that cannot be placed are removed as casualties.  Armor saves may not be taken.  
* **TANK SHOCK**: Models which are driven over suffer an automatic Strength 8 hit with no armor save which inflicts **MASSIVE WOUNDS**.  
* **RAMMING**: If the target of the Ram is not a Superheavy Vehicle, after determining Strength, but before determining Armour Penetration, roll on the following table:

| D6 | Result |
| :---: | :---- |
| 1 | **NO EFFECT**: Carry on with the remainder of the Ram as normal. |
| 2-5 | **KERR-SMASH**: The Superheavy Vehicle adds D6 to the armor penetration roll for the ram. |
| 6 | **FLIPPED**: The vehicle being rammed scatters D6” and then suffers an EXPLODES\! result from the Penetrating Hit table. |

**Superheavy Walkers in Melee**
Superheavy Walkers are massive war engines whose sheer scale makes positional warfare against them a meaningful tactical consideration. Unlike standard Walkers, which are small enough that facing in close combat is abstracted to the front armor, Superheavy Walkers use the standard vehicle facing rules in melee combat.
When a Superheavy Walker is hit by a melee attack, determine the facing hit using the attacking model's position relative to the Superheavy Walker, as described in STEP 1: DETERMINE VEHICLE FACE HIT. This replaces the standard Walker rule that all melee attacks hit the FRONT ARMOR.
* **Grenades against Superheavy Walkers:** Grenade attacks against non-immobilized Superheavy Walkers hit on a 4+ instead of the standard 6+ for regular Walkers.
All other Walker melee rules — including determining close combat results, damaged Walker penalties, and voluntary withdrawal — still apply to Superheavy Walkers as normal.


## **INDEPENDENT CHARACTERS** {#independent-characters}

Models with the **INDEPENDENT CHARACTER** type are special in that they are allowed to **JOIN** other units. 

* **JOINING UNITS**: Independent characters can join a unit and will function as part of that unit for movement, coherency, shooting, leadership, assaulting, and other purposes.    
  * Independent Characters may NOT join units that include vehicle models or monstrous creatures models.  
* **JOINING DURING DEPLOYMENT**: Independent characters may be **JOINED** to a unit prior to deployment.  If the joined units were placed in reserve, they count as a single unit when rolling to enter play from reserve.  
  * If either unit has any special deployment rules (infiltrator, deep strike, etc.) in order for that ability to be used both the unit and the independent character must have the same ability.  
* **JOINING & LEAVING A UNIT**: During the **MOVEMENT** phase independent characters may join or leave friendly units as follows:  
  * May **JOIN** a unit by moving within coherency of the unit and declaring they wish to join the unit after completing their move.  The joined unit may not move after being joined by the independent character.  
  * May **LEAVE** a joined unit by declaring they wish to leave before moving the unit or character.  
  * A character may not join and leave (or leave and join) a unit in the same turn.  
  * An independent character cannot leave a unit that is **engaged in melee**, **BROKEN**, or **PINNED.**
  * **Independent Characters and Unit Type Differences**: An Independent Character whose unit type differs from the unit they have joined (for example, a Cavalry character joining an Infantry unit) moves at their own unit type's speed. The unit must maintain coherency at the end of the move as normal. See **MOVEMENT PHAS**E for rules on mixed-type units.
* **DURING SHOOTING**: Independent characters joined to a unit may always declare and resolve shooting against a different target than the joined unit, whether or not the joined unit SPLITS FIRE.  
* **DURING ASSAULT PHASE**:  
  * If joined to a unit, independent characters must charge with that unit and get into base-to-base contact with an enemy model if possible.  
  * When in a melee engagement, they are treated as part of the unit and allocate their attacks and can be allocated wounds as if they were a normal model in the unit.  
  * Independent characters may make pile-in moves first (instead of using the closest model) to ensure that they are able to stay engaged in the melee fight.  
* **ALLOCATING WOUNDS:** When a unit that has been joined by independent characters suffers an unsaved wound, those wounds do NOT have to be allocated to an already wounded independent character (circumventing the normal rule for applying unsaved wounds to wounded models first).  
* **SHOOTING INDEPENDENT CHARACTERS**: Independent characters that have not joined a unit may only be selected as a target for shooting attacks if at least one of the following situations apply:  
  * The independent character is within 18” AND is the closest figurine model to the attacker.  
  * The independent character is more than 18” away AND more than 6” from a friendly figurine unit.  
  * The independent character is a monstrous creature or flying monstrous creature.
