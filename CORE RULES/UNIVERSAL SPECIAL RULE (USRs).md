# **UNIVERSAL SPECIAL RULES**

### Movement/Deployment

* **Acute Senses**: Unit can re-roll which random table edge it arrives on from reserves.  
* **Assault Vehicle**: Passengers disembarking from an assault vehicle do not lose their extra attack charge bonuses when disembarking and charging from an assault vehicle. All open-topped vehicles count as Assault Vehicles.  
* **Crusader**: Roll extra dice when advancing and use the highest.  Adds D3” to pursuit rolls.  
* **Fleet**: Unit may still charge into melee combat even if it advanced this turn.  
* **Hit & Run**: Unit may choose to leave combat at the end of the assault phase after taking break tests. Take an initiative test. If passed, the unit moves 3D6” in a chosen direction. The unit does not count as having broken (or fallen back), and any now unengaged enemy units they moved away from may only consolidate 3”.  
* **​​Immobile:** A unit with this rule cannot move once it has entered play, and if it is a vehicle it counts in all respects as having suffered an Immobilised result that cannot be repaired in any way. This does not cause it to lose a Structure Point.  
* **Infiltrate**: During deployment, may be set up after normal deployment. The unit must be set up 12" or more away from any enemy units that do NOT have line of sight and 18” or more away from enemy units WITH line of sight. The unit also gains **Outflank**.  
* **Jink:** Units that moved in the prior turn can activate their jink ability  as a reaction to being shot and gain a 5+ cover save until the start of its next turn. If a model with this rule used **turbo-boosters** or moved at flat out speeds, it gains a 4+ cover save instead. Models that utilize a jink only shoot with snap fire on their next turn.  
* **Move Through Cover**: Roll a 3D6 for difficult terrain tests (instead of 2D6) and choose the highest.  
* **Outflank:** When units with this ability enter from reserve, they have a chance to enter from the side table edge. On a 1-2, enters from player’s left, 3-4 on players right, 5-6 players choice of side edge. May alternatively choose to enter from reserve normally from the player’s normal table edge(s).  
* **Power of the Machine Spirit**: A vehicle moving less than flat out speed and not using smoke launchers can fire one additional main weapon at full Ballistic Skill.  
* **Rage:** A unit with this special rule gains \+2 Attacks, instead of the usual \+1, when it charges into close combat.  Additionally, if there is a valid enemy unit within charge range, this unit must charge that target in the Assault phase.  
* **Relentless**: Model may move and fire heavy, ordanance, salvo, or rapid fire weapons as if they remained stationary.  May still charge even after firing such weapons.  
* **Scouts**: May always be deployed. Before rolling to see who goes first, may take a bonus normal move.  Confers scout ability to a dedicated transport it is mounted in. If both players have scouts, roll-off and alternate moving scouts starting with the winner of the roll-off. The unit also gains **Outflank**.  
* **Skilled Rider**: Re-roll failed dangerous terrain tests. Gains \+1 to any Jink saves  
* **Slow and Purposeful**: Has the ***Relentless*** special rule and always moves as if in difficult terrain  
* **Swift:** Units composed entirely of swift models, fall back 3D6” instead of 2D6”.  During pursuit and retreat moves, they move their Initiative Value \+ 2D6”.  
* **Turbo-Boosters**: Instead of a normal move, unit may turbo-boost up to 24". Turbo-boosting units may not shoot in the shooting phase or charge in the assault phase. Units that also have the **Jink** rule gain a 4+ cover save if they used a Turbo-Boost move in their last movement phase.

### Unit Properties

* **Adamantium Will:** \+1 bonus to deny the witch and the unit may attempt to deny the witch even if they are not a psyker themselves.  
* **And They Shall Know no Fear**: Automatically passes regroup tests at the start of the turn and may be used normally for the rest of the turn. If caught by a pursuit, they automatically regroup and are re-engaged in close combat, and do not suffer extra wounds from being caught in a pursuit.  Immune to **Fear**.  
* **Brotherhood of Psykers**: Unit counts as Mastery Level 1 Psyker. Uses the unit’s highest leadership value for psychic power tests.  
* **Bulky**: Counts as two models for transport capacity.  Very bulky counts as three models.  Extremely bulky counts as five.  
* **Counter-Attack**: If this unit is not engaged with any enemy models and an enemy unit successfully completes a charge move against it, the unit may take a Leadership test. If passed, this unit gains \+1 attack during the Assault phase.  
* **Daemon**: Model gains a 5+ invulnerable save and causes **Fear.** Additionally, units entirely composed of models with this special rule automatically pass **Pinning Tests** and **Casualty Tests**, and are immune to the effects of **Suppression Tokens** (they do not accrue them). When a unit with this rule loses a melee engagement, it does not take a standard **Break Test**. Instead, it takes a **Daemonic Instability Test**. This is a Leadership test, subject to the standard Break Test modifiers (e.g., -1 for every three wounds lost by). Daemonic Instability Tests are not subject to the Insane Heroism rule. A roll of double 1s on a Daemonic Instability Test does not trigger Insane Heroism; the test is instead resolved normally, and any relevant wargear effects (such as the Daemonic Icon) apply as written.
  * **If passed:** The unit remains locked in combat.
  * **If failed:** The unit does not become **Broken** and does not **Retreat**. Instead, the unit suffers 1 unsaved wound for every point the test was failed by. No saving throws of any kind (including invulnerable or *Feel No Pain*) may be taken against these wounds. If models survive, the unit remains locked in combat.* **Drop Pod (X”)**: When a model with this rule arrives via Deep Strike, any unit with models within X” of its hull/base must take a pinning test.  
* **Eternal Warrior**: Immune to massive wounds. Model suffers 1 wound instead of D3 wounds.  
* **Fear:** Enemy units in base contact must pass a Leadership test before resolving their close combat attacks.  If the test fails, those units attack with WS1 in the melee engagement.  
* **Fearless**: Automatically pass all Morale and Pinning tests.  May not **Take Cover\!** or choose to voluntarily fail a break test.  
* **Feel No Pain:** A model with Feel No Pain that fails their normal saving throw, may attempt to ignore the wound a second time with Feel No Pain.  Roll a D6 and if the value is equal to or above the Feel No Pain value the wound is ignored.  Unless otherwise specified, the wound is ignored on a 5+.  If the model would receive the Feel No Pain rule from multiple sources, it improves its Feel No Pain roll by 1 (5+ becomes 4+, etc.) for every source of Feel No Pain after the first.  Feel No Pain rolls may never be improved beyond 2+.  
* **Furious Charge**: Models gain \+1 strength and \+1 initiative in assault on the turn they charged.  
* **Hammer of Wrath (X)**: On the turn this unit charges, models may perform X number of their normally allowed attacks at initiative 10 instead of during their normal initiative step.   
* **Hatred**: If a unit with hatred is engaged in melee combat against a hated enemy type, and was NOT engaged in melee combat with that enemy type during the prior turn, the unit may re-roll all failed close combat to-hit rolls against the hated target.   
* **It Will Not Die:** Roll a D6 at the start of its turn for each model with less than its starting wounds but is not destroyed.  On a 5+ it regains one lost wound. A vehicle with It Will Not Die may, on a 5+, repair an Engine Damaged, Weapon Destroyed, or Immobilized damage result.    
* **Monster Hunter:** A unit that contains at least one model with this special rule re-rolls all failed To Wound rolls against Monstrous Creatures.  
* **Night Vision**: Units containing at least one model with this effect ignore night fighting restrictions.   
* **Psyker (MLX):** A unit with this Special Rule can cast **Psychic Powers** and **Deny the Witch**.  Their Mastery Level is noted in the X position.  
* **Preferred Enemy (x)**: The unit can re-roll failed hits against the indicated preferred enemy.  
* **Rare Unit (0-X):** A unit with this special rule may only be taken up to the number listed in the X position in a detachment.  If a unit can be upgraded to a differently named profile (Space Marine Captain to Chapter Master for example) taking a unit with one of those profiles counts against the limit for the whole unit and thus both profiles.  
* **Resistant to Small Arms:** The model gains an unmodified Feel No Pain (5+) roll against unsaved wounds caused by attacks from weapons that do not have blast/template and are not a Primary Weapon.  
* **Shrouded**: Adds \+2 to the value of cover saves conferred by terrain (Max of 2+ cover save).  If in the open, receives a 5+ cover save. Does NOT stack with stealth.  
* **Stealth**: Adds \+1 to the value of cover saves conferred by terrain (Max of 2+ cover save).  If in the open, receives a 6+ cover save. Does NOT stack with shrouded.  
* **Stubborn**: Ignore negative leadership modifiers on Morale and Pinning tests.  
* **Swarms**: Have ***Stealth**, **Move Through Cover**, **Vulnerable to blasts/templates***, and ***Resistant to Small Arms***.  
* **Tank Hunters**: \+1 to armor penetration rolls.  Automatically passes tank shock morale tests.  
* **Unique Unit:** A unit with this special rule may only ever be taken once across an entire army regardless of how many detachments are in the army.  
* **Vulnerable to Blasts/Templates**: Each hit counts as 1D3 hits from blast/template weapons  
* **Zealot:** A unit with at least one model with this effect gains **Fearless** and **Hatred**.

### Attack/Weapon Properties

* **Armorbane**: Attack rolls a 2D6 for armor penetration.  
* **Backblast:** When a weapon with ***Backblast*** shoots, place a template with the small end in contact with the rear of the weapon (if the firing model is not a vehicle, you may instead use base contact) of the firing model, and the large end pointing 180° from the target.  If the weapon is a blast weapon, determine the location of the large end before scattering the blast template.  Any models wholly under the template take a S3 AP- hit.   
* **Blind**: Unit HIT by a weapon with **blind** must take an initiative test.  If failed, all models are reduced to BS1 and WS1 until the end of their next turn.  
* **Concussive**: A model suffering an unsaved wound is reduced to Initiative 1 until the end of the following assault phase. Against units with no Initiative (i.e. vehicles) inflicts crew shaken if hit.  
* **Defensive Grenade:** If a unit equipped with a weapon with this Special Rule was unengaged at the start of the current assault phase and charged this turn, all charging units lose their bonus attack for charging.  When a unit armed with a grenade weapon makes a Shooting attack, a single model may choose to throw a grenade instead of making any other shooting attack  
* **Deflagrate:** For each model slain by a weapon with this rule, the target unit suffers one additional automatic hit resolved at the same Strength and AP. These bonus hits do not themselves Deflagrate.  
* **Disintegrating Beam:** When firing a weapon with this special rule, a To Wound roll of a 6 wounds automatically, regardless of the target’s Toughness. Against vehicles and buildings, an Armour Penetration roll of a 6 that does not cause a penetrating hit automatically causes a glancing hit.
* **Double Weapon:** Weapon counts as being equipped with two melee weapons for determining extra attacks granted for wielding two melee weapons.  
* **Fleshbane:** Always wounds on a 2+.  No effect versus vehicles.  
* **Force**: Count as a ***Power Weapon***.  Additionally, a **PSYKER** wielding a force weapon may make a psychic test after inflicting one or more unsaved wounds.  If the test is passed, each unsaved wound deals a massive wound (D3 damage).  
* **Gets Hot**: If a model shooting this weapon rolls 1 to hit, it causes an automatic wound with no AP on that model and must take an armor or invulnerable save. If the save fails, the shooting model suffers a wound. Vehicles are not affected by Gets Hot.   
* **Graviton**: The to-wound roll is instead equal to the model’s armor save roll. Against vehicles, instead of rolling for armor penetration, roll a d6.  On a 1-5 nothing happens. On a 6, the vehicle becomes immobilized.  
* **Grenade:** When a unit armed with a grenade weapon makes a Shooting attack, a single model may choose to throw a grenade instead of making any other shooting attack.  
* **Ignores Cover**: Weapon negates any cover saves.  
* **Interceptor**: A weapon with this rule may be fired at an enemy unit immediately after that enemy unit enters from reserves and ends the movement phase within line of sight and range of the weapon. If used in this way, the firing model may not shoot in the shooting phase of its following turn.  
* **Haywire:** Instead of normal armor penetration, roll dice. 1 \= no effect, 2-5 \= Glancing hit; 6 \= penetrating hit  
* **Lance:** Treat vehicle armor greater than 12 as a 12\.  
* **Laser Lock:** If a model is firing one or more weapons with this special rule and also one or more other weapons, roll To Hit with the weapon(s) with the Laser Lock special rule first. If the Laser Lock weapon(s) causes one or more hits, treat all weapons on the same model yet to fire this phase as being twin-linked for the rest of the phase. Note that all of the hits caused by the firer’s shooting attacks are still resolved simultaneously.  
* **Master-Crafted**: Re-roll one failed to-hit roll per turn.  
* **Melta**: When making armor penetration rolls, roll a 2D6 instead of a D6 if the target is at half range or less.  
* **Monster Hunter**: Units containing at least one model with this effect rerolls failed to wound rolls against monstrous and gargantuan creatures.  
* **One Use:** A weapon with this special rule may only be fired once per game. After firing, the weapon is expended and may not be used again for the remainder of the battle. Mark the weapon as expended after use.  
* **Orbital:** If an arrow is rolled on the scatter dice when firing a weapon that has this special rule, the shot always scatters the full 2D6" regardless of the firing model’s Ballistic Skill. In addition, firing an Ordnance weapon that has this special rule does not prevent the firing model from declaring a charge against the target unit in the same turn.  
* **Pinning** \- Unsaved wounds caused by this weapon force the unit to take a Pinning Test (see **PINNING**).  
* **Poison:** Wounds on a fixed number regardless of strength (i.e. 4+). If the attacker's strength is higher than the defender's toughness, re-roll failed wounds. Poison weapons have no effect against vehicles. Default value is 4+.  
* **Power Weapon:**  When resolving an attack with this special rule, if the weapon's AP value is equal to the target's armor save value, the target suffers an additional \-1 modifier to their armor save roll (for a total of \-2). Invulnerable saves may be taken as normal.  
* **Precision Shots:** When rolling a D6 to determine which unit is hit while **Shooting Into Melee**, add \+1 to the result.  
* **Rampage**: Models gain \+D3 attacks if the enemy units contain more models than friendly models that are locked in close combat.  
* **Rending** \- On a wound roll of a 6, causes automatic wound and the weapon is treated as AP2 for ranged attacks and a power weapon attack for melee attacks. Against vehicles, a penetration roll of a 6 adds an additional D3 to the result of the penetration roll.  
* **Smash:** Melee attacks are treated as power weapons (i.e. reduce armor saves). Additionally, this unit may choose to exchange all of its normal  attacks to conduct a single smash attack instead.  This is resolved at double strength (up to max of 10).  
* **Shred**: Re-rolls failed to wound rolls.  
* **Soul Blaze:** Units wounded by this attack are set ablaze.  Roll D6 at the end of each turn.  On a 4+, the unit takes D3 S4 AP5 hits with no cover saves.  If less than 4, the blaze goes out and the effect is removed.  
* **Specialist Weapon:** Specialist weapons do not grant a \+1 bonus for wielding two melee weapons unless the two melee weapons are the exact same specialist weapon.   
* **Split Fire**: If a unit has this listed as a *special rule* rule, it automatically passes any of its split fire tests and may also split its fire between up to three (instead of two) different targets.  
* **Sniper:**  Always wounds on 4+. Gains ***Precision Shots***. Additionally, if a 6 is rolled to hit, roll D6.  If the result is 6, no further roll is required for Shooting into Melee to determine which unit is hit, and the owner of the firing model may assign wounds resulting from that specific hit, instead of the owner of the target unit.  
* **Strikedown**: Models suffering one or more wounds from this weapon (even if saved) move as if in difficult terrain on its next turn.  
* **Terrible Weapon:** A weapon with this special rule always inflicts a massive wound per successful hit.  Against vehicles they may reroll on the armor penetration table but must take the new roll even if it is worse.  
* **Torrent:** Place the flame template in any orientation so long as the template is entirely within 12” range and the tip of the narrower end is closer to the firing model than the tip of the wider end.  
* **Twin-Linked**: Re-roll failed to-hit rolls with this weapon, and generates an additional hit roll for each 6 rolled during the initial to-hit rolls (re-rolled hits do not generate extra hits on a 6).  Additional hits generated in this way do not re-roll failed to-hit rolls. Does not count as two weapons. Twin-linked ***Template*** weapons automatically hit all models fully or partially under the template.   
* **Two-Handed:** Model attacking with this weapon does not receive a bonus attack for having additional close combat weapons.  
* **Unwieldy**: Model attacking with this weapon is at Initiative 1 unless it’s a monstrous creature or vehicle walker.  
* **Vortex:** A weapon with this special rule uses a blast marker of some type (e.g. blast, large blast, massive blast, etc). Place the appropriate marker, roll for scatter and apply damage. For determining Wound allocation, always assume the shot is coming from the center of the marker, in the same manner as a Barrage weapon.  The marker for a Vortex weapon is not removed from play after damage has been resolved. Leave it in play on the tabletop. The marker is impassable terrain as long as it remains in play.  At the beginning of every subsequent player turn, the marker scatters 2D6" (reroll the scatter die if you roll a hit). If a double is rolled, the marker is removed from play instead. Any unit under the marker’s new location is hit. Apply damage as described in the weapon’s profile.  
* **Witchblade:** Always wounds on a 2+.  Count as Strength 9 against vehicles.

### Flyer Related

* **Missile Lock:** Missiles fired by this unit scatter D6” rather than 2D6”.  
* **Skyfire:** Models shooting with this effect use their normal Ballistic Skill when shooting at flyers, flying creatures, and skimmers, and can only snap fire at other targets.  
* **Strafing Run:** When shooting Assault/Heavy/Rapid Fire/Salvo weapons at ground units (without flyer or skimmer type) this vehicle has \+1 BS and all shots count as pinning.  
* **Supersonic**: Moving at Flat Out speed must move at least 18” and up to a max of 36”. Cannot enter hover mode.  
* **Vector Dancer**: Can make an additional pivot on the spot up to 90-degrees at the end of its move. Cannot move at flat out speed in the following turn.  
* **Vector Strike**: Nominate an unengaged model this unit has moved over.  That unit takes D3+1 hits resolved at the model’s unmodified strength and AP3.  Against vehicles hits are resolved on the side armor. A model making a vector strike counts as having moved for shooting.
