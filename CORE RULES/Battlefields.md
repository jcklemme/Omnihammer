### Battlefields

* **BATTLEFIELD SIZE:** The standard table size for a battlefield is 6-feet by 4-feet (72” x 48”). Depending on the size of the game and/or the mission that is chosen, a different battlefield size may be recommended.  
* **TABLE EDGES**: The outer boundary of the battlefield is called a **TABLE EDGE**.  Some rules may reference the long or short table edges, dimensions relative to table edges, or table edges associated with a specific player (which is usually defined in the mission description).

### Preparing for Battle

The following procedure is used for determining the mission, setting up the board, and deploying forces.

1. **CHOOSE MISSION:** Use the mission selection charts (see [MISSION BRIEFINGS](https://docs.google.com/document/u/0/d/1brLLro_8XAutunsTkNYz9BMnfEV41GwNj4alB9WGvGI/edit)) to determine what mission will be played based on the point value of the game.  Alternatively, if both players agree they may select a mission archetype of their choice or use another method.  
2. **SETUP TERRAIN:** Arrange terrain on the battlefield (See TERRAIN SETUP). Review the selected mission to ensure there are no special terrain setup requirements.  
3. **DETERMINE MISSION PARAMETERS:** Follow the **mission briefing** to finalize the mission parameters and setup.  This typically includes the following items (although the order may vary depending on the mission):  
   1. Determine Mission Approach  
   2. Determine Scoring Conditions  
   3. Determine Deployment Zone Layout and Layout of Objectives  
   4. Determine Sides / Select Deployment Zones. Players roll-off, and the winner may choose to select deployment zones (and whether they are the attacker or defender) or allow their opponent to select deployment zones.  
4. **PICK WARLORD TRAIT:** Players select their Warlord and assign a Warlord Trait to them from either the Core Rules or their Codex.  
5. **PLACE UNITS IN RESERVE:** Players may place units in reserve, as allowed by the unit’s special rules and/or the mission’s deployment rules (see RESERVES).  
6. **DEPLOY UNITS:** The player that chose sides and/or deployment zone begins deploying first. Players deploy their remaining units (those not in reserve) following the procedure identified below (see DEPLOYMENT)  
7. **DETERMINE FIRST PLAYER:** Players roll off, and the winner may choose whether they want to go first or second. The player going second gains a number of **SECOND PLAYER CONCESSIONS** depending on the size of the game (see CONCESSIONS).  If one player’s army is wholly painted and abides by WYSIWYG and the other player’s does not, there is no need to roll off as described in the Models section of the rules.

### Terrain Setup {#terrain-setup}

Terrain features provide obstacles for units to move around, cover, means of blocking line of sight, and disrupting movement patterns.  The following guidelines should be followed when setting up terrain on the battlefield:

#### **1\. SELECT TERRAIN**

* A minimum of 25% of the battlefield should be covered by terrain. It is recommended that at least a third (33%) of the battlefield be covered in terrain, and no more than two-thirds (66%).  
* Before setting up terrain players should alternate selecting terrain features from the available supply until approximately one third to one half of the table area can be covered by terrain.

#### **2\. DEFINE TERRAIN**

After selecting terrain, players should clearly define the classification of each terrain piece. These include:

* **DENSE COVER/AREA TERRAIN**: Provides a cover save for models within or behind the terrain piece up to the terrain’s height.  Blocks LoS automatically beyond 6” of depth into the terrain piece up to their terrain’s height.  
* **COVER TYPE:** Soft, Hard, or Fortified cover \- determine level of cover save provided.  
* **DIFFICULT TERRAIN:** Affects movement of units traveling through the terrain piece  
* **DANGEROUS TERRAIN**: Models moving through this type of terrain risk suffering damage  
* **STRUCTURES:** Terrain features such ruins, buildings, scaffolding, or other multi-leveled structures that allow units to be placed on different floors and/or inside of them.  Define points of access and means of moving between levels.  
* **OBSTACLES:** Sand bags, trenches, and other barriers that can provide intervening terrain.  
* **IMPASSIBLE TERRAIN:** Terrain features, or portions of terrain, where models cannot move onto or through.

#### **3\. PLACE TERRAIN**

* **OBJECTIVE SPECIFIC TERRAIN:** If the mission rules require the placement of certain types of terrain features in certain positions, players alternate placing such terrain features on the board first, before placing other terrain.    
* **STANDARD PLACEMENT METHOD:** Once terrain is selected, players then roll-off and the winner chooses one piece of terrain and places it on the table.  Then the other player chooses and places a piece of terrain. Continue alternating back and forth until all pieces of terrain have been placed.  
* **OTHER METHODS:** Players may decide together to use a different method for placing terrain if they desire so long as both players agree to the approach.    
  * An alternate method is to have one player completely set up the terrain, and the other player then automatically wins the die rolls for choosing which side of the board to deploy their forces.
