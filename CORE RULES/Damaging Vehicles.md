### Damaging Vehicles {#damaging-vehicles}

The process for damaging vehicles is different than it is for figurine models.  Instead of rolling to wound and taking a saving throw, the attacker will roll to see if their attack penetrates the vehicle's armor, and if it does, what type of damage is incurred on the vehicle. Follow the steps below when a vehicle is hit.

#### **STEP 1: DETERMINE VEHICLE FACE HIT**

* When a vehicle is hit by an attack, first determine the facing (i.e. side) that is hit. The facing of a vehicle hit by an attack depends on the attacking model's position relative to the orientation of the vehicle, as follows:  
  * A model wholly within the front arc (or touching the forward arc line) hits the front armor.  
  * A model wholly within the side arc (or touching the rear arc line) hits the side armor.  
  * A model wholly within the rear arc hits the rear armor.  
* Facing applies to ranged and melee attacks.  
* Visually extend (or use a tape measure or stick) the facing arc lines out from the vehicle to determine facing.   
* If different models in an attacking unit fall within different arcs, the attack rolls for the different groups must be distinguished.   
* **Open-topped vehicles** hit with a template weapon are subject to the [**NO ESCAPE**](#buildings-&-fortifications) rule.


#### **STEP 2: VEHICLE ARMOR PENETRATION ROLL**

* Once the facing hit is determined, the attacking player makes a penetration roll by rolling a D6 and adding the attack’s strength to the die result.  Compare the result to the armor value of the vehicle on the facing that was hit on the chart below:

| PENETRATION RESULT VS. HIT FACE’S ARMOR VALUE | RESULT... |
| :---- | :---- |
| Penetration result is **LOWER** than the hit armor value | **No damage** |
| Penetration result **EQUALS** the hit armor value | **Glancing Hit** |
| Penetration is **GREATER than** the hit armor value | **Penetrating Hit** |

A number of modifiers affect the results of the vehicle penetration roll:

* **AP “ \- “ WEAPONS:** Ranged attacks with an AP “ \- “ value (i.e. no AP) treat all penetrating hits on the above table as glancing hits instead.  
* **HULL DOWN**: If a vehicle is **OBSCURED** behind terrain from the point of view of a shooting model, the vehicle gains a cover save depending on the type of terrain (see chart above under [COVER SAVES](#cover-saves)). If the save is successful, penetrating hits become glancing hits, and glancing hits become no damage.  
* **SKIMMERS:** Skimmers moving at FLAT OUT speeds gain a 5+ cover save. If this save is successful, penetrating hits become glancing hits, and glancing hits become no damage.


#### **STEP 3: ROLL FOR DAMAGE**

* Once a vehicle suffers a glancing or penetrating hit, the attacking player rolls on the appropriate chart (glancing hit or penetrating hit) below to determine the resulting damage.  
* A number of modifiers affect the vehicle damage roll.  Apply any of these after rolling the damage dice.  
  * **AP 1 WEAPONS**: Add \+1 to the damage result  
  * **OPEN-TOPPED VEHICLES**: Add \+1 to the damage result  
  * **ORDNANCE WEAPONS**: Roll a 2D6 and pick the highest result  
  * **PRIOR DAMAGE**: \+1 to the damage result if the vehicle has suffered any number of prior **engine damage, immobilized, or weapon destroyed** results due to an earlier shooting attack or a melee attack occurring at an earlier initiative step during an engagement. Damage incurred by the unit currently shooting or during the current initiative step of an engagement does not count as prior damage.

| GLANCING HIT |  |  | PENETRATING HIT |  |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1 | **Crew/Systems shaken**: fires with snap fire |  | 1 | **Crew/Systems stunned**: can’t move, fires with snap fire |
| 2 | **Crew/Systems shaken**: fires with snap fire |  | 2 | **Crew/Systems stunned**: can’t move, fires with snap fire |
| 3 | **Crew/Systems stunned**: can’t move, fires with snap fire |  | 3 | **Engine damaged** **\+ crew shaken** |
| 4 | **Engine damaged**: Halve movement speeds |  | 4 | **Weapon destroyed** **\+ crew stunned** |
| 5 | **Weapon destroyed**: attacker chooses a weapon |  | 5 | **Immobilized \+ crew stunned** |
| 6 | **Immobilized**: can no longer move or pivot |  | 6 | **Wrecked**: vehicle destroyed (leaves wreck) |
| 7+ | **Wrecked**: vehicle destroyed (leaves wreck) |  | 7+ | **EXPLODES\!** |

* **CREW SHAKEN**: The vehicle may only fire with snap fire until the end of its next turn.  
  * Embarked passengers firing out of firing ports or open-topped vehicles also fire with snap fire.  If passengers disembark, they are still subject to snap fire.  
* **CREW STUNNED**: The vehicle counts as shaken (as above) and may not move in its next movement phase.   
  * Embarked passengers may disembark as normal but may not advance.  They may shoot, but only do so with snap fire.  
* **ENGINE DAMAGED**: The movement distances of the vehicle are halved at each speed level. Each subsequent engine damaged result further halves movement speed.  
* **WEAPON DESTROYED**: A weapon of the attackers choice is destroyed and can no longer be used. Each time this result occurs, a different weapon may be destroyed.  
* **IMMOBILIZED**: The vehicle may no longer move or pivot in place.  
  * **SKIMMERS**: Immobilized skimmers that moved over 12” in their prior movement phase are WRECKED.  
* **WRECKED**: The vehicle is destroyed but remains on the board and counts as a piece of terrain.   
  * **EMBARKED PASSENGERS:** Must pass a PINNING TEST or become pinned,  even if normally immune to pinning tests.  
* **EXPLODES\!** Remove the vehicle and replace it with a similarly-sized crater, which provides hard cover for models within the crater.  Additionally, all models within D6” of the vehicle's hull take an automatic Strength 4 hit.  Roll to wound and take saving throws as normal (cover saves may be taken).  
  * **EMBARKED PASSENGERS:** Automatically PINNED (even if normally immune to pinning). Additionally, each passenger model suffers an automatic hit that wounds on a 4+.  Armor saves may be taken as normal.

### Vehicle Structure Points

Vehicles, depending on their overall level of armoring, will have a number of structure points (SPs).  So long as the SPs last, they are automatically used to reduce critical damage that a vehicle takes, keeping it in the fight a little longer.

* **NUMBER OF STRUCTURE POINTS:** Vehicles have a number of structure points based on the total of its three armor values.  Total up the front, rear, and side (just one side) armor and consult the chart below:

| VEHICLE ARMOR VALUE (Front \+ Rear \+ One Side) | Structure Points (SPs) | Examples of  Armor Profiles |
| :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 30 | **0** | 10/10/10 |
| 31 \- 34 | **1** | 11/11/10, 12/12/10, 13/11/10 |
| 35 \- 39 | **2** | 12/12/12, 13/12/10, 14/13/10 |
| 40 \- 41 | **3** | 14/14/12, 14/13/13 |
| 42 or more | **4** | 14/14/14 |

* **USING STRUCTURE POINTS**: When a vehicle suffers a wrecked or explodes result on the damage table, subtract a structure point from the vehicle and apply a \-1 modifier to the damage result sufficient to reduce the result to a non-destroyed result.  
  * **JURY-RIG**: Whenever a vehicle suffers a damage result the owning player MAY choose to spend remaining structure points to further reduce a damage result.  
  * **FLYERS/SKIMMERS**: Flyers or fast moving skimmers that moved in their prior turn such that an immobilized result would cause the unit to be destroyed must also use structure points (if available) to reduce the damage result to below immobilized if possible (See [Flyers](#flyers)).
