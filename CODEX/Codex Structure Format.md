# OmniHammer Codex Structure Writing Guide

**Version:** 0.1  
**Purpose:** This document defines the standard chapter structure, ordering, and content expectations for all OmniHammer faction codexes. The goal is a consistent reading experience across factions where a new player can start at Chapter 1 and move through the codex in sequence to learn how to build and play their army.

---

## Guiding Principle: Natural Teaching Flow

The codex is ordered so that each chapter builds on the last. A reader should never encounter a term, mechanic, or reference that hasn't been introduced in an earlier chapter. The sequence moves from broad faction identity → specific rules → army construction framework → equipment and options → individual units → reference material.

---

## Chapter Order

| # | Chapter | Required? | Contents |
| :---: | :---- | :---: | :---- |
| 1 | Lore Introduction | Yes | Faction overview, "How to Use This Codex" |
| 2 | Faction Special Rules | Yes | Universal rules shared by all units in the faction |
| 3 | Subfactions | Yes | Subfaction-specific rules, drawbacks, force org modifications, and wargear |
| 4 | Force Organization | Yes | Standard FOC, faction-specific FOC modifications, special detachments/formations |
| 5 | Psychic Disciplines | Conditional | Faction-specific psychic powers; only included for factions with psykers |
| 6 | Wargear | Yes | All equipment, armor, upgrades, and relics/named wargear |
| 7 | Weapon Profiles | Yes | All weapon stat profiles, grouped by weapon category |
| 8 | Warlord Traits | Yes | Warlord Trait table and descriptions |
| 9 | Unit Datasheets | Yes | All generic unit entries, organized by FOC slot |
| 10 | Named / Special Characters | Yes | All named character datasheets, grouped by subfaction |
| 11 | Summary Appendix | Yes | Consolidated statline tables by model name (no points) |

---

## Chapter Specifications

### Chapter 1 — Lore Introduction

**Purpose:** Set the tone and identity of the faction. Orient the reader on how the codex is structured.

**Contents:**

- A light lore overview of the faction — who they are, how they fight, and their place in the 41st millennium. This is not a full history section; it is a flavor introduction to establish context for the rules that follow.
- A brief **"How to Use This Codex"** section that explains the chapter flow and what the reader will find in each chapter. This helps new players navigate the document and understand why the chapters are ordered the way they are.

**Guidance:**

- Keep the lore concise. This is a rules document with flavor, not a lore document with rules.
- The "How to Use This Codex" section should be a short paragraph or bulleted walkthrough, not a detailed explanation of every mechanic.

---

### Chapter 2 — Faction Special Rules

**Purpose:** Introduce the universal mechanics that define the faction's identity on the tabletop. Every unit (or nearly every unit) in the codex will reference these rules.

**Contents:**

- All special rules that are universal or near-universal across the faction (e.g., ***And They Shall Know No Fear***, ***Mob Rule***, ***Voice of Command***).
- Faction-wide resource systems (e.g., **WAAAGH! Energy**, **Orders**).
- Faction-wide restrictions or allowances (e.g., **Volkite Limit**, **Relic of the Past**).
- Any faction-specific vehicle or model type rules that apply broadly (e.g., ***Ramshackle***).

**Guidance:**

- This chapter answers: "What makes this faction mechanically different from every other faction?"
- Rules listed here should apply to most or all units. If a rule only applies to a single unit or a small subset, it belongs on that unit's datasheet instead.
- Complex systems (like WAAAGH! Energy or the Orders system) should be fully defined here with all sub-rules, tables, and interactions. The reader should not need to look elsewhere to understand how these systems work.
- Cross-reference the Core Rules when a faction special rule modifies or overrides a core mechanic. Use the format: *"This is an exception to the normal [RULE NAME] rules."*

---

### Chapter 3 — Subfactions

**Purpose:** Present the subfaction options that the player will choose when building their army. Each subfaction modifies the faction's baseline identity established in Chapter 2.

**Contents:**

- An introductory note explaining that each detachment must choose one subfaction, and how subfaction selection interacts with detachment construction.
- Each subfaction entry, containing:
  - **Special Rules** — the unique rules conferred by this subfaction (e.g., signature rule, drawback).
  - **Force Organization Modifications** — any changes to the standard FOC or additional restrictions/allowances (e.g., "may take one Heavy Support without using a slot," "must take at least two Elites before any Fast Attack").
  - **Subfaction-Specific Wargear** — any wargear options unique to this subfaction. List availability and costs here; the wargear's rules/profile should be defined in Chapter 6 (Wargear) or Chapter 7 (Weapon Profiles) as appropriate.

**Guidance:**

- Each subfaction entry should follow a consistent internal structure: Special Rules → Force Organization Modifications → Subfaction Wargear. This makes it easy to compare subfactions.
- The subfaction's drawback (if any) should be clearly labeled and immediately follow the signature rule. Drawbacks are a core OmniHammer design principle — subfactions should create genuine trade-offs, not incidental bonuses.
- Force Organization modifications listed here take precedence over the standard FOC in Chapter 4 when the relevant subfaction is selected.

---

### Chapter 4 — Force Organization

**Purpose:** Define how the player constructs their army list using the Force Organization Chart.

**Contents:**

- The faction's standard Force Organization Chart (or a reference to the Core Rules standard FOC if the faction uses it without modification).
- Any faction-specific FOC modifications that apply universally (not tied to a specific subfaction — those belong in Chapter 3).
- **Special Detachments and Formations** — alternative FOC structures the faction may use (e.g., Artillery Battery Detachment, Armored Company, Mechanized Company). Each special detachment should include:
  - Its unique FOC slot structure.
  - Any special rules that apply when using this detachment.
  - Any restrictions on which units may be included.

**Guidance:**

- The reader has already seen the subfaction Force Org modifications in Chapter 3. This chapter covers the baseline FOC and any broader structural options.
- Special detachments are included here rather than as a separate chapter — they are alternative ways to organize an army and belong with the primary organizational framework.
- If the faction uses the Core Rules standard FOC without modification, state this explicitly rather than reprinting the chart. A cross-reference is sufficient.

---

### Chapter 5 — Psychic Disciplines (Conditional)

**Purpose:** Define faction-specific psychic powers available to the faction's psykers.

**Contents:**

- Each psychic discipline available to the faction, with:
  - The discipline name.
  - The Primaris power (if applicable).
  - Each power's name, warp charge cost, type (blessing, malediction, witchfire, etc.), and full rules text.
- Notes on which psyker units may access which disciplines.

**Guidance:**

- This chapter is **only included for factions that have psyker units.** If a faction has no psykers (e.g., Tau, Necrons), omit this chapter entirely. The chapter numbering for subsequent chapters should adjust accordingly — do not leave a gap.
- If a faction's psykers only use disciplines from the Core Rules (e.g., Divination, Telepathy) with no faction-specific additions, this chapter may be omitted with a note in the Faction Special Rules chapter directing the reader to the Core Rules.

---

### Chapter 6 — Wargear

**Purpose:** Define all equipment, armor, upgrades, and relics available to the faction's units.

**Contents:**

- **Equipment and Upgrades** — all non-weapon wargear items, organized by category. Suggested categories (adapt per faction):
  - Armor
  - Special Issue Wargear / Personal Upgrades
  - Support Equipment
  - Movement and Deployment Equipment
  - Vehicle Upgrades
  - Standards and Banners
- **Relics and Named Wargear** — unique, limited-availability items (e.g., Heirlooms of Conquest, Chapter Relics). Include availability restrictions (e.g., "one per army," "HQ only").
- **Wargear Cost Summary** — a consolidated list of all wargear items and their point costs.

**Guidance:**

- This chapter defines what wargear *does*. Weapon stat profiles (Range, S, AP, Type) belong in Chapter 7 (Weapon Profiles), not here — unless the wargear item is not a weapon (e.g., Refractor Field, Medi-Pack, Teleport Homer).
- Wargear items that are also weapons (e.g., a Power Fist has both a rules description and a stat profile) should have their rules description here and their stat profile in Chapter 7. Cross-reference between the two chapters.
- Subfaction-specific wargear availability is noted in Chapter 3 (Subfactions), but the wargear's actual rules and cost are defined here.
- Relics are included in this chapter rather than as a separate chapter — they are wargear with additional restrictions.

---

### Chapter 7 — Weapon Profiles

**Purpose:** Provide a consolidated reference for all weapon stat profiles used by the faction.

**Contents:**

- All weapon profiles, grouped by **weapon category**. Suggested categories (adapt per faction):
  - Bolt Weapons
  - Plasma Weapons
  - Flame Weapons
  - Melta Weapons
  - Laser Weapons
  - Volkite Weapons
  - Missile Weapons
  - Sonic Weapons
  - Ordnance Weapons
  - Exotic / Faction-Specific Weapons
  - Melee Weapons (by type: Power Weapons, Chain Weapons, Force Weapons, etc.)
  - Grenades
- Each profile should include: Name, Range, S, AP, Type, and any special rules or notes.
- Lore/flavor text for each weapon is encouraged but should follow the stat profile, not precede it.

**Guidance:**

- Weapon categories should be adapted to the faction. Not every faction will have every category (e.g., Orks don't have Volkite weapons). Add faction-specific categories as needed (e.g., Sonic Weapons for Chaos, Pulse Weapons for Tau).
- Within each category, weapons should be ordered from lightest/smallest to heaviest/largest (e.g., Bolt Pistol → Boltgun → Storm Bolter → Heavy Bolter).
- This chapter is a **reference chapter** — it is not meant to be read cover-to-cover. Its purpose is to be flipped to when reading a datasheet that references a weapon.
- Combi-weapons and twin-linked variants should appear alongside their base weapon category.

---

### Chapter 8 — Warlord Traits

**Purpose:** Define the Warlord Trait options available to the faction's warlord.

**Contents:**

- The faction's Warlord Trait table (typically D6 or D3+3 format, or a pick-list).
- Each trait's name, full rules text, and any restrictions on which models may use it.
- Notes on any subfaction-specific Warlord Traits or restrictions (e.g., "If your Warlord is from [subfaction], they must/may take [specific trait]").

**Guidance:**

- This chapter sits immediately before the datasheets. By this point, the reader understands the faction rules, their subfaction, how to organize their army, what wargear and weapons exist, and (if applicable) what psychic powers are available. The last thing they need before choosing units is how to customize their warlord.
- If specific named characters have mandatory warlord traits, note that here with a cross-reference to the Named Characters chapter.

---

### Chapter 9 — Unit Datasheets

**Purpose:** Present all generic unit entries for the faction, organized by Force Organization slot.

**Contents:**

- All generic (non-named) unit datasheets, in the following FOC slot order:
  1. **HQ**
  2. **Troops**
  3. **Elites**
  4. **Fast Attack**
  5. **Heavy Support**
  6. **Lord of War**
  7. **Dedicated Transports**
  8. **Fortifications**

- Each datasheet should include (in order):
  - Unit name and FOC slot designation
  - Stat table (WS, BS, S, T, W, I, A, Ld, Sv, Inv, Type)
  - Unit composition and base points cost
  - Special rules
  - Default wargear
  - Options (upgrades, weapon swaps, transport options)
  - Designer's notes (where relevant, explaining the intent behind mechanical choices)

**Guidance:**

- Within each FOC slot, units should be ordered from most common/core to most specialized. For example, under Troops: Tactical Marines before Scouts before Crusader Squads.
- Dedicated Transports appear at the end of the datasheets chapter, even though individual unit entries may reference them in their options. This avoids scattering transport datasheets across multiple FOC sections.
- Point costs appear on the datasheet. This is the authoritative location for points — they do not appear in the Summary Appendix.
- Datasheets should reference wargear and weapons by name only — do not reprint weapon profiles or wargear rules on the datasheet. The reader should use Chapters 6 and 7 as reference.

---

### Chapter 10 — Named / Special Characters

**Purpose:** Present all named character datasheets, grouped by subfaction.

**Contents:**

- Named characters organized into subfaction groups, with a **"General"** (unaligned) group at the top for characters not tied to a specific subfaction.
- Internal ordering within the chapter:
  1. **General / Unaligned** — named characters available to any subfaction.
  2. **[Subfaction A]** — characters tied to this subfaction.
  3. **[Subfaction B]** — and so on, in the same order subfactions appear in Chapter 3.

- Each named character datasheet follows the same format as generic datasheets (stat table, composition, special rules, wargear, options, designer's notes).

**Guidance:**

- Named characters are separated from generic datasheets because they serve a different purpose in list-building. Generic datasheets are the building blocks; named characters are the customization layer.
- Named characters tied to a subfaction should note any restrictions (e.g., "May only be taken in a detachment with [Subfaction] Chapter Tactics").
- If a named character replaces a generic unit entry (e.g., "One Company Command Squad may replace its Commander with Colonel Straken"), state this clearly in the named character's composition line.

---

### Chapter 11 — Summary Appendix

**Purpose:** Provide a quick-reference table of all model statlines in the codex.

**Contents:**

- A consolidated statline table listing every model profile in the codex, organized **alphabetically by model name** (e.g., "Space Marine," "Space Marine Sergeant," "Veteran Sergeant").
- Columns: Name, WS, BS, S, T, W, I, A, Ld, Sv, Inv.
- Vehicle statlines in a separate table: Name, WS, BS, S, AVF, AVS, AVR, I, A, SP.

**Guidance:**

- **Do not include point costs in the appendix.** Points are on the datasheets (Chapters 9 and 10) and only there.
- This appendix is a reference tool for mid-game lookups, not a list-building tool. Its purpose is to let a player quickly check a statline without flipping through dozens of datasheets.
- Model names should match exactly how they appear on the datasheets. Do not abbreviate or rename.

## Formatting Conventions

All codex content must follow the formatting conventions defined in **CLAUDE.md**:

| Element | Format | Example |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |

| Key Terms | `**BOLD UPPERCASE**` | **UNIT** |
| Rule Cross-References | `**BOLD UPPERCASE**` | See **COHERENCY** |
| Important Notes | `**Bold sentence.**` | **Models may not move through enemy models.** |
| Optional Rules | Blockquote with prefix | `> **OPTIONAL RULE! RULE NAME**` |
| Tables | Standard Markdown tables | — |

## Conditional Chapters

Some chapters are not required for every faction. When a conditional chapter is omitted, subsequent chapter numbers should **not** shift — leave no gaps in numbering. Instead, if Chapter 5 (Psychic Disciplines) is omitted, Chapter 6 becomes the fifth chapter in the document, but its content remains "Wargear." The chapter numbers in this guide refer to content identity, not literal numbering in every codex.

**Alternatively**, codexes may use descriptive chapter titles without numbers (e.g., "Wargear" instead of "Chapter 6 — Wargear"). This avoids numbering inconsistencies across factions. **[Decision needed — numbered vs. titled chapters.]**

## Cross-Reference Expectations

The codex structure is designed so that forward references are minimized. However, some are unavoidable:

- **Subfactions (Ch. 3)** may reference subfaction-specific wargear whose rules are defined in **Wargear (Ch. 6)** or **Weapon Profiles (Ch. 7)**.
- **Datasheets (Ch. 9)** will reference wargear by name, pointing the reader to **Wargear (Ch. 6)** and **Weapon Profiles (Ch. 7)**.
- **Named Characters (Ch. 10)** may reference subfaction rules defined in **Subfactions (Ch. 3)**.

These forward references are acceptable because the reader has encountered the framework (faction rules, subfactions, FOC) before reaching the reference material (wargear, weapons) and the units that use them.

## Notes on Existing Codexes

The following existing OmniHammer codexes were written before this guide was established and will need structural revision to conform:

- **Space Marines** — closest to the guide structure but needs chapter reordering (Warlord Traits and Named Characters need separation; weapon profiles need reorganization by category).
- **Orks** — follows the general flow but has wargear and datasheets interleaved in a single document; needs chapter separation.
- **Chaos** — uses a numbered section system (1. Detachment Factions, 2. Faction Rules, etc.) that partially aligns; needs Named Characters separated and weapon profiles extracted.
- **Imperial Guard** — Faction Rules and Force Organization are partially drafted; needs full structural pass.
- **Imperial Knights** — compact codex; may not need all chapters (e.g., no Psychic Disciplines, limited subfaction count).
- **Tau** — Support Systems and datasheets drafted; needs structural alignment.
