# **CHAOS SPACE MARINE SPECIAL RULES**

### **Veterans of the Long War**

Automatically passes regroup tests at the start of the turn and may be used normally for the rest of the turn. If caught by a pursuit, they automatically regroup and are re-engaged in close combat, and do not suffer extra wounds from being caught in a pursuit.    
**Immune to *Fear***. Confers ***Hatred (Space Marines)*** and **\+1 Ld**.    
Additionally, allows the model with this special rule to purchase upgrades from the Veteran Abilities list.

* ***Daemon***

* ***Rage***

### **The Blood God’s Due**

***The Blood God’s Due:*** In the **ASSAULT PHASE,** if a unit entirely composed of ***Daemon of Khorne*** models with this special rule wipes out an enemy unit and performs an **OVERRUN\!** consolidation, add \+1" to the result. If the die roll is a 1, the unit immediately suffers D3 automatic hits resolved at the unit's majority Strength and AP.

# **FORCE ORGANIZATION**

A Chaos army is constructed using one or more **DETACHMENTS.** When adding a **DETACHMENT** to your army, you must select its **DETACHMENT FACTION.** This dictates the core units available, how it interacts with the **FORCE ORGANIZATION CHART**, and the specific special rules it utilizes.

* **Chaos Space Marines:** The elite Traitor Legions and renegade chapters.   
* **The Lost and the Damned:** Massive hordes of mortal cultists, mutants, and traitor Astra Militarum.  
* **Daemonic Incursion:** Pure manifestations of the warp. Unlike Daemons taken as reserves in other detachments, units in this detachment are not required to use the Daemonic Summoning rule and may deploy normally or use standard RESERVES.  
* **Dark Mechanicum:** The corrupted tech-priests of the Eye of Terror. This detachment prioritizes heavy armor, WALKERS, and DAEMON ENGINES.  
* **Traitoris Lance (Chaos Knights):** A specialized super-heavy detachment representing a fallen Knight house or dreadblades. Its Force Organization Chart consists of: 1 Mandatory HQ (Must be a Knight Character), 1-4 Optional Troops (War Dogs only), and 0-2 Optional Lord of War (Standard Knights).

### **Daemons in Detachments**

For all Detachments that **are not** Daemonic Incursion Detachments, units that contain models with the ***Daemon***, ***Daemon of Khorne, Daemon of Nurgle, Daemon of Slaanesh,*** or ***Daemon of Tzeentch*** special rules may not be included unless the model has the ***Daemonic Possession*** or ***Daemonic Summoning*** special rule.  If a daemon model does not have one of these rules, it is restricted to Daemonic Incursion Detachments.

## **Daemonic Incursion**

*The presence of daemons in realspace is an open wound in the fabric of reality. The longer they persist — and the more violence erupts around them — the thinner the veil becomes. Reality buckles. The air screams. And from the widening rift, more horrors pour through.*

A **Daemonic Incursion Detachment** follows all the normal rules for Force Organization except only units consisting entirely of models with the ***Daemon***, ***Daemon of Khorne, Daemon of Nurgle, Daemon of Slaanesh,*** or ***Daemon of Tzeentch*** special rules may be taken in the detachment.  
Additionally, A **Daemonic Incursion Detachment** uses the following rules in this section.  
Units in a Daemonic Incursion Detachment ignore the restrictions of the ***Daemonic Summoning*** and ***Daemonic Possession*** special rules.

**Restriction:** The following rules apply only to units in a Daemonic Incursion detachment. Daemons taken as allies in another detachment do not benefit from these rules (they are bound by Daemonic Summoning instead, representing a more controlled and limited manifestation).

### **The Warp Tide**

The daemon player tracks their current **Warp Tide** level on a counter or die visible to both players. The Warp Tide represents how thoroughly the barrier between realspace and the Immaterium has been compromised.  
The **Warp Tide** level starts at 0 at the beginning of the game.

**Generation:**

| Triggering Event | Warp Tide Gained |
| :---: | :---: |
| Start of Daemon player turn | **\+1** |
| A friendly Daemon unit completely destroys an enemy unit | **\+1** |
| A friendly Daemon unit is completely destroyed | **\+1** |
| The Daemon **Warlord** slays an enemy **CHARACTER** in a **Heroic Duel** | **\+1** |

**Rules:**

* Warp Tide may never exceed 12\.  
* Warp Tide never decreases  
* Warp Tide is generated immediately when a triggering event occurs.

### **Warp Rift Zones**

*Reality buckles and distorts in the presence of daemonic entities. Mortals who stray too close feel their courage waver, the ground crack beneath their feet, and the air itself reject their presence.*

Each unit composed entirely of models with the ***Daemon*** USR in a Daemonic Incursion detachment generates a **Warp Rift Zone** — an aura extending outward from the unit. The size and intensity of this zone scales with the current Warp Tide level:

| Warp Tide | Zone Radius | Effects on Enemy Units within the Zone |
| :---: | :---: | ----- |
| **0-2** | **3”** | **Whispers of the Warp:** \-1 Ld |
| **3-5** | **6”** | **The Veil Thins:** \-1 Ld, Zone counts as **Dangerous Terrain** for enemy models |
| **6-11** | **6”** | **Reality Fractures:** \-2 Ld, Dangerous Terrain, Enemy units must pass a **Leadership Test** to us **Stand & Shoot\!** and **Return Fire\!** Reactions.  If failed, the reaction is lost (unit does not fire) |
| **12** | **9”** | **The Warp Made Manifest:** \-2 Ld, Dangerous Terrain, Leadership Tests for reactions.  Enemy models that start their turn wholly within the Zone suffer a S4 AP- hit. |

**Rules:**

* Leadership penalties from multiple overlapping Warp Rift Zones **do not stack**.  
* Dangerous Terrain from Warp Rift Zones is tested when enemy models move through or end their move within the Zone.  
* Models with the ***Daemon*** USR are never affected by Warp Rift Zones.  
* The Leadership test for reactions uses the unit’s modified Leadership (after the Zone’s Ld penalty).  The test is taken before any firing is resolved.  If passed, the reaction proceeds as normal.  If failed, the reaction is lost and the unit does not fire.  
* The hits from **Warp Made Manifest** are resolved at the beginning of the enemy player’s turn, before movement.

### **Daemonic Resurgence**

*Daemons are not truly slain — they are merely banished back to the Immaterium, where they coalesce, reform, and claw their way back through the wound in reality. The wider the rift, the faster they return.*

**Resurgence (Ongoing Units)**  
At the start of each daemon player's Movement Phase, before any units move, perform a **Resurgence Check** for each friendly daemon unit on the battlefield that has lost models but is not destroyed:

| Warp Tide | Model Returns On: |
| :---: | :---: |
| **0-2** | **No Resurgence** |
| **3-5** | **6+** |
| **6-9** | **5+** |
| **10-12** | **4+** |

Roll a D6 for each slain model from that unit. If the roll meets the threshold on the table at the left, that model is returned to the unit.  
**Rules:**

* Returned models are placed in unit coherency with surviving models, as close to the unit as possible  
* Returned models count as having moved this turn (they may not shoot heavy weapons at full BS, etc.)  
* Returned models may act normally in subsequent phases (they may charge, fight in melee, etc.)  
* A model may only be returned via resurgence once per turn  
* Resurgence is rolled before any other start-of-turn effects.

**Daemonic Reformation (Destroyed Units)**  
**Warp Tide 7+ Required, Once Per Game**

If a friendly daemon unit has been completely destroyed, the daemon player may declare a **Daemonic Reformation** at the start of their Movement Phase. The destroyed unit is placed in Ongoing Reserves at its minimum starting unit size (e.g., 8 Bloodletters with no upgrades). It arrives next turn via Deep Strike, using the standard Daemonic Summoning placement rules (within 6" of a friendly model with a Personal Icon, no scatter). If no friendly model with a Personal Icon is on the battlefield when it arrives, the unit is destroyed per the normal Daemonic Summoning consequence.

**Rules:**

* Daemonic Reformation may only be used once per game, regardless of how many units have been destroyed.  
* The reformed unit returns at minimum unit size with default wargear only (no upgrades).  It counts as a new unit with the same profile as the destroyed unit.  
* The reformed unit counts toward the army’s unit count for victory conditions  
* The reformed unit benefits from all **Daemonic Incursion** rules (Warp Rift Zones, Resurgence) upon arrival.
