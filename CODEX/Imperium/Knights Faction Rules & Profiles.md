# QUESTOR MECHANICUS FACTION RULES

## Questor Mechanicus

The Knights of the Questor Mechanicus bear a different bond than their Imperialis counterparts. Where feudal chivalry drives the knight-scions of the Imperium, cold purpose drives these warriors. They march to secure sacred technologies, to protect Forge Worlds, and to enact the calculated will of the Adeptus Mechanicus with an efficiency that their feudal cousins often mistake for heartlessness. They are not heartless. They simply have no use for emotion that does not serve the Omnissiah.

Models with the **QUESTOR MECHANICUS** special rule are bound to a specific Forge World through ancient oaths of mutual survival and technological dependency. All models with this rule benefit from their detachment's chosen **HOUSEHOLD TRADITION** and their detachment's **FORGE WORLD BOND**. In addition, all models with the **QUESTOR MECHANICUS** special rule have the following special rules:

- ***Machine Communion***
- ***Cold Intellect***

### Machine Communion

The Throne Mechanicum of a Questor Mechanicus Knight does not merely pilot the war machine — it communes with it. The pilot and machine exist in a state of constant diagnostic feedback, enabling rapid repair of damaged systems even in the heat of battle. To a Mechanicus pilot, this is not resourcefulness. It is religious obligation.

At the start of each **MOVEMENT PHASE**, a model with ***Machine Communion*** may attempt to repair one damaged system. Roll a D6: on a **5+**, remove one **Engine Damaged**, **Weapon Destroyed**, or **Immobilized** result currently affecting this model. ***Machine Communion*** may not restore lost **STRUCTURE POINTS**.

Certain **HOUSEHOLD TRADITIONS** and **FORGE WORLD BLESSINGS** improve the required roll or provide additional repair benefits.

### Cold Intellect

The Throne Mechanicum of a Questor Mechanicus house is engineered to suppress volatile emotional states in the pilot, replacing passionate impulse with cold, efficient computation. The pilot does not feel fear. They perform threat assessment. The distinction, to the Mechanicus, is everything.

A model with ***Cold Intellect*** is immune to the ***Fear*** special rule and automatically passes all **PINNING TESTS**. When this model is required to take any morale test, negative Leadership modifiers originating from enemy special rules (such as ***Fear***, ***Terror***, or equivalent effects) do not apply. Modifiers from the model's own condition — such as being at reduced strength or out of coherency — still apply normally.

**A model with *Cold Intellect* may never choose to voluntarily fail a morale test.**

## Ion Shield

The Ion Shield is the Knight's greatest defense and its most closely guarded secret — a directional energy barrier projected from the shield-bearing arm of the war machine. The Throne Mechanicum constantly monitors shield integrity and adjusts generator output in real time. Sustained fire will eventually saturate the emitters, but a well-positioned Knight can present its strongest face to the enemy and shed all but the heaviest blows.

A model equipped with an **ION SHIELD** gains an invulnerable save against all ranged and melee attacks. The strength of this save depends on the origin point of the attack relative to the Knight's facing arcs:

| Attack Origin | Ion Shield Save |
| :---- | :---- |
| Within the model's **FRONT ARC** | **3+** |
| Within the model's **SIDE** or **REAR ARC** | **4+** |

**Ion Shields have no effect against attacks with the *Vortex* special rule.**

### Overload Tokens

Sustained fire can saturate an Ion Shield's emitter array, degrading and ultimately collapsing the barrier. Each time a model with an Ion Shield **fails an Ion Shield save**, place one **OVERLOAD TOKEN** on the model. Track Overload Tokens with a die or token visible to both players.

The current number of Overload Tokens affects the Ion Shield as follows:

| Overload Tokens | Shield Status | Effect |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **0–1** | **Stable** | Ion Shield operates normally |
| **2** | **Strained** | Ion Shield save is worsened by 1 (3+ becomes 4+; 4+ becomes 5+) |
| **3+** | **Overloaded** | Ion Shield provides no invulnerable save whatsoever |

### Recovery

**Passive Recovery:** At the start of each of the model's **MOVEMENT PHASES**, remove one Overload Token from the model.

**Emergency Field Regeneration:** At the start of the model's **SHOOTING PHASE**, before declaring any targets, the controlling player may declare that this model is performing an Emergency Field Regeneration. Remove all Overload Tokens from the model. The model may make no shooting attacks this turn.

## The Forge World Bond

Every Knight House of the Questor Mechanicus exists in a relationship of mutual dependency with its bonded Forge World. The Forge World provides technological support, replacement components, and the sacred rites of maintenance that keep the Knight suits operational across centuries. In return, the Knight House pledges its blades and cannons to the Forge World's defense and expeditionary mandates. This bond shapes everything — the Knight's maintenance rituals, its deployment doctrine, and the specific blessings the Forge World's Magi can confer upon a house's greatest champion.

### Selecting a Forge World Bond

When constructing a **QUESTOR MECHANICUS** detachment, the controlling player must declare a **FORGE WORLD BOND** for that detachment. All models in the detachment with the **QUESTOR MECHANICUS** rule must share the same Forge World Bond.

A player may select a **NAMED HOUSE** from the list below, using the pre-defined Household Tradition and Forge World Blessing associated with that house. Alternatively, they may field a **CUSTOM HOUSE** by selecting one option from the **HOUSEHOLD TRADITIONS** list and one option from the **FORGE WORLD BLESSINGS** list.

## Household Traditions

All models in a detachment with the **QUESTOR MECHANICUS** rule gain their detachment's **HOUSEHOLD TRADITION**.

---

### House Taranis — *Bonded to Mars*

**RITES OF THE SACRED MACHINE**

The Knights of House Taranis are the oldest and most revered of the Questor Mechanicus, bound to the Red Planet itself. Their maintenance rites are performed according to STC protocols so ancient that even senior Magi treat them with reverence. The machine spirits of House Taranis are extraordinarily cooperative — as if the suits themselves wish to endure, to serve, to persist. A Taranis Knight that falls is a greater loss than most Forge Worlds can calculate.

This model's ***Machine Communion*** repair roll is improved to **4+** instead of 5+. Additionally, when this model uses **Emergency Field Regeneration**, it may simultaneously remove one **Engine Damaged**, **Weapon Destroyed**, or **Immobilized** result without making a roll.

---

### House Raven — *Bonded to Metalica*

**IRONCLAD EFFICIENCY PROTOCOLS**

House Raven's doctrine is the doctrine of Metalica itself — relentless advance, overwhelming suppression, continuous fire. A Raven Knight does not slow its advance for the convenience of its targeting systems. It improves both simultaneously. The Forge World has invested considerable resources into ensuring this is not merely an aspiration but a measurable operational fact.

This model adds +2" to its movement at **COMBAT SPEED**. When this model moves at **CRUISING SPEED**, it may fire its Main Weapons using **SNAP FIRE** rather than being prohibited from firing Main Weapons entirely. Defensive weapons may still be fired normally at Cruising Speed.

---

### House Mortan — *Bonded to Graia*

**UNYIELDING CALCULUS**

House Mortan and Forge World Graia share a cold and absolute conviction: ground taken is ground kept. A Mortan Knight holding an objective is a mathematical certainty. It will not yield. The calculation does not permit it. Other houses speak of honor and courage. House Mortan speaks of optimal positional efficiency and acceptable casualty thresholds. The outcomes are often identical, and Mortan Knights find this unremarkable.

This model has the ***Stubborn*** special rule. When this model is within 3" of an objective marker, it counts as two models for the purposes of scoring and contesting that objective. When this model suffers a **CREW SHAKEN** or **CREW STUNNED** damage result, roll a D6; on a **4+**, the result is ignored.

---

### House Krast — *Bonded to Forge World Lemenates*

**PREDATOR ALGORITHMS**

The Magi of Forge World Lemenates have long specialized in the analysis of biological and mechanical threat profiles, cataloguing the vulnerabilities of enemy war machines and monstrous creatures with the same dispassionate thoroughness applied to any other engineering problem. House Krast's Knights are configured for lethal efficiency against exactly the targets the Forge World most values destroying.

This model has the ***Monster Hunter*** and ***Tank Hunters*** special rules. When this model makes ranged attacks against a **VEHICLE** or **MONSTROUS CREATURE**, it may re-roll all failed armor penetration rolls.

---

### Custom House

A Custom House selects one of the following Traditions for its detachment:

| Tradition | Effect |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Data-Hunger Protocols** | When this model destroys an enemy **VEHICLE** or **MONSTROUS CREATURE**, the controlling player immediately gains 1 Strategy Point (this cannot exceed the army's starting Strategy Point total). |
| **Isotopic Targeting Arrays** | This model ignores cover saves when making ranged attacks. All ranged attacks made by this model have the ***Pinning*** special rule. |
| **Seismic Stabilization Rites** | This model may fire all weapons as if it remained stationary regardless of how far it moved this turn. This model may not make **FLAT OUT** moves. |
| **Aggressor Interrupt Subroutine** | This model may fire Overwatch at its full Ballistic Skill rather than snap fire. This model may make Overwatch reactions even if it moved during its prior Movement Phase. |
| **Blessed Ferromantic Shielding** | All of this model's Armor Values are increased by +1 (maximum 14). When this model suffers a Glancing Hit, apply a -1 modifier to the damage roll result. |
| **Arcane Locomotive Rites** | This model has the ***Scouts*** special rule and gains the ***Outflank*** ability. This model may re-roll failed **DANGEROUS TERRAIN TESTS**. |

## Forge World Blessings

When a Knight House's greatest champion is identified before a major campaign, the bonded Forge World's ruling Magos performs a formal Rite of Designation — a sacred ceremony in which additional maintenance rites are conducted, select machine spirit configurations are unlocked, and a formal Mandate is inscribed into the Warlord Knight's Throne Mechanicum. These blessings reflect the Forge World's own priorities and areas of technological mastery.

The controlling player must designate one model in a **QUESTOR MECHANICUS** detachment as the **WARLORD KNIGHT** before the game begins. The Warlord Knight receives the **FORGE WORLD BLESSING** corresponding to its detachment's Forge World Bond.

A **CUSTOM HOUSE** selects one Blessing from the named options below.

---

### Blessing of Mars

*The Fabricator-General's inner circle has taken personal interest in this Knight's campaigns. Its machine spirit has been recalibrated at the highest level of the Martian priesthood, its repair rituals elevated to sacred scripture.*

Once per game, when this model would suffer a **WRECKED** or **EXPLODES!** result on the Vehicle Damage Table and before spending any Structure Points, roll a D6. On a **3+**, reduce the damage result by 2 steps (for example: **EXPLODES!** becomes **Weapon Destroyed + Crew Stunned**; **WRECKED** becomes **Immobilized**). Additionally, while this model has fewer Structure Points remaining than it started the game with, its ***Machine Communion*** repair roll is improved to **3+**.

---

### Blessing of Metalica

*Metalica's combat Magi have tuned this Knight's targeting systems beyond standard operational parameters. Its weapon spirit and optical cogitators operate in perfect synchronization, each output refined to a precision that most Forge Worlds consider wasteful. Metalica considers it optimal.*

This model has **+1 Ballistic Skill**. This model generates **OVERLOAD TOKENS** only when it fails Ion Shield saves against **PENETRATING HITS** — failed saves against Glancing Hits do not generate Overload Tokens. When this model fires at two different targets in the same Shooting Phase via **SPLIT FIRE**, it may re-roll all failed To Hit rolls against the second declared target.

---

### Blessing of Graia

*Graia's gift is certainty of position. The Warlord Knight is designated a Territorial Anchor — its presence creates a zone of calculated fire dominance that enemy commanders cannot safely ignore.*

This model has the ***Interceptor*** special rule. This model may never be the target of **TANK SHOCK**. Once per game, at the start of any Shooting Phase, before either player shoots, this model may perform a single **Priority Overwatch** — it fires as if in Overwatch at any one enemy unit within range and line of sight, regardless of whether that unit is charging. This attack is resolved using the model's full Ballistic Skill.

---

### Blessing of Lemenates

*The Magi of Lemenates designate this Knight the supreme predator of the engagement. Its targeting mandates are updated in real time with the highest-priority threat profiles in the Forge World's war-database. It does not seek glory. It fulfills its mandate.*

Once per game, at the start of a Shooting Phase or Assault Phase of the controlling player's choice, this model may enter **PEAK PREDATOR CALCULUS**. For the duration of that phase, this model may re-roll all To Hit rolls and all failed armor penetration rolls against a single nominated enemy **VEHICLE** or **MONSTROUS CREATURE** target. Declare the nominated target and activate **PEAK PREDATOR CALCULUS** before any dice are rolled.

# DESIGN NOTES

*This section documents design decisions and rationale for future reference.*

### Questor Mechanicus Identity

The two universal rules — ***Machine Communion*** and ***Cold Intellect*** — are intended to make the faction feel meaningfully different from Questor Imperialis even before any Household Tradition is applied. Cold Intellect is deliberately not Fearless (Knights can still break if the math is bad enough) but it strips out all the emotional manipulation effects that enemies might use to engineer those breaks. Machine Communion creates a passive repair loop that rewards the opponent for focusing fire to force multiple damage results faster than the Knight can clear them.

### Ion Shield Design

The hybrid overload system rewards smart positioning (front arc 3+ vs. other arcs 4+) while creating a meaningful decision tree for both players. The attacker must decide whether to concentrate fire to generate Overload Tokens — accepting that they're hitting the strongest save — or flank to hit weaker saves but slower token generation. The Emergency Field Regeneration creates a high-stakes turn where the Knight goes cold (no shooting) but resets entirely. Passive recovery (one token per turn) means a Knight that survives a turn of heavy fire will slowly recover, but sustained pressure over multiple turns will keep it strained.

### Household Tradition Differentiation

The four named houses are designed around distinct battlefield roles: House Taranis (survivability and repair), House Raven (aggressive advance), House Mortan (objective control and durability), House Krast (heavy target specialist). Custom house options in the table provide additional archetypes for players who want to build thematically distinct forces.

### Forge World Blessings vs. Household Traditions

Traditions are army-wide (all Knights in the detachment), making them affect overall strategy. Blessings affect only the Warlord Knight, making them feel like a personal designation rather than a doctrinal difference. This separation is intentional — it means the Warlord Knight feels specially commissioned by the Forge World while the rest of the household operates on standard Forge World doctrine.
