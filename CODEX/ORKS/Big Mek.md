### Personal Equipment (Additions)

| Wargear | Effect |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Iron Gob** | Model gains **+1 Leadership**. Only available to models with the Ork Nob, Warboss, or Big Mek profile (or higher). |
| **Shield** | Model gains a **6+ invulnerable save**. A model with a Shield may not use two-handed weapons or any weapon that requires both hands. |
| **Fight Detecta** | *Blood Axes Characters only.* A model equipped with a Fight Detecta can use it in place of making a shooting attack. If he does so, target an enemy unit within 12" (this does not count as choosing a target for his unit to shoot at). A unit that is targeted by one or more Fight Detectas has its cover saves reduced by 1 until the end of the phase. |

**Iron Gob:** These massive facial appliances are bolted onto an Ork's armour, or onto the Ork's jaw itself. They exaggerate the already fearsome appearance of these green brutes and are often viewed as symbols of authority. As such, they are most commonly found on Ork Nobz and Warbosses. The sight of a massive iron-jawed Ork bellowing orders tends to keep even the most unruly boyz in line.

**Shield:** Little more than a metal plate strapped to an Ork's arm, allowing him to block an enemy's blows in close combat and shelter behind it when shot at. It is rarely used, as most Orks would prefer to carry one more weapon into combat rather than bother about their protection. A model carrying a Shield cannot use any weapon with the ***Two-Handed*** special rule — the shield arm is occupied.

**Fight Detecta:** A discarded Imperial auspex found by the Orks of the Blood Axes Klan. It comes in handy for Greenskins who want to find a fight more quickly — when an enemy is nearby, it lights up and makes noises, much to the Orks' delight. Mechanically functions identically to an Imperial Auspex — because that is exactly what it is. The Orks just gave it a better name.

### Big Mek Equipment (New Section)

| Wargear | Effect |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Big Mek's Tools** | At the start of the movement phase, the bearer may attempt to repair one friendly **VEHICLE** within 2". Roll a D6: on a **4+**, repair one **Immobilized**, **Weapon Destroyed**, or **Engine Damaged** result. On a **5+**, the bearer may instead restore 1 lost **STRUCTURE POINT**. The bearer may not shoot in the same turn they attempt a repair. |
| **Electric Armour** | *Big Mek only.* At the start of each Assault Phase in which the bearer is engaged in close combat, the bearer may activate the Electric Armour. **All** models in base-to-base contact with the bearer (friend and foe, including models from the bearer's own unit) suffer a S4 AP6 hit. These hits are resolved before any other attacks are made. The bearer may not activate Electric Armour in the same turn they charged (the capacitors need time to build charge). |

**Big Mek's Tools:** A Big Mek's personal toolkit — far more extensive and sophisticated than the standard Mekboy's kit. Heavy-duty welding rigs, plasma cutters, mag-clamps, kustom diagnostic gubbinz, and a truly bewildering collection of spanners. Where an ordinary Mek might coax a damaged engine back to life with a well-placed kick, a Big Mek can strip and rebuild a gearbox under fire. The improved repair rolls (4+/5+ versus the standard 5+/6+) reflect years of experience and better equipment.

**Electric Armour:** Worn by Ork Mekboyz, this crude but effective device consists of heavy capacitor banks wired directly into the wearer's armour plates. When activated, it discharges a massive electrical shock through the armour's surface, electrocuting anything in contact — friend and foe alike. The Mek doesn't care. The capacitors require a full round to build sufficient charge, which is why the armour cannot be activated on the same turn the wearer charges into combat. The indiscriminate nature of the discharge means that any boyz fighting alongside the Mek had better keep their distance — or accept a few thousand volts as the price of standing next to a Big Mek in a scrap.

### Grenades (Addition)

| Weapon | Range | S | AP | Type |
| :---- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---- |
| **Super Stikkbomb** | 8" | 8 | 2 | Assault 1, Grenade, One Use, Gets Hot, Blast |

**Super Stikkbomb:** An "upgraded" version of a Krak Stikkbomb — simply put, a large number of Krak Stikkbombs wired together, designed to make the biggest bang possible. Invented by the Mekboyz and a lot more dangerous and liable to kill the user as well as destroy whatever vehicle they are chucked at. ***Gets Hot*** represents the very real probability that the wiring shorts out and detonates the entire bundle in the thrower's hands. ***Blast*** represents the enormous explosion radius of multiple shaped charges detonating simultaneously. You only get to throw this once — ***One Use*** — assuming you survive the attempt.

### Vehicle Melee (Addition)

| Weapon | Range | S | AP | Type |
| :---- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---- |
| **Grabbin' Klaw** | — | 8 | 3 | Melee |

**Grabbin' Klaw:** A massive hydraulic grabbing arm mounted on the front of Ork vehicles. Designed to seize enemy vehicles, infantry, and anything else in its path and either crush them or hurl them aside.

***Grab an' Drag:*** After a vehicle equipped with a Grabbin' Klaw completes a Ram or Tank Shock move, the controlling player may nominate one enemy model that was in the path of the Ram/Tank Shock. That model must pass a Strength test (roll equal to or under their Strength on a D6) or be grabbed. A grabbed model is dragged D6" in the direction the vehicle moved, then placed in base contact with the vehicle's final position. The grabbed model suffers a S8 AP3 hit. If the model survives, it is placed in base-to-base contact with the vehicle and counts as being engaged with it. Vehicles that are grabbed instead suffer a S8 AP3 hit against their side armour — they are not moved.

### Vehicle-Mounted Twin Weapons (New Entries)

| Weapon | Range | S | AP | Type |
| :---- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---- |
| **Twin Big Shoota** | 36" | 5 | 5 | Heavy 3, Twin-Linked |
| **Twin Rokkit Launcha** | 24" | 8 | 3 | Assault D3, Twin-Linked |

**Twin Big Shoota:** Two Big Shootas bolted together in a side-by-side or over-under configuration. The twin-linked configuration compensates for the poor aim of the Ork crew — the sheer volume of fire means something is bound to hit.

**Twin Rokkit Launcha:** Paired Rokkit Launchas mounted on a shared firing bracket. Still fires the same wildly variable salvo (D3 shots) but the twin-linked configuration allows the crew to re-roll misses. Commonly mounted on Mekboy Junkas and heavier Ork vehicles.

### Vehicle Turret Weapons (New Sub-Section)

| Weapon | Range | S | AP | Type |
| :---- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---- |
| **Supa-Skorcha** | Template | 6 | 3 | Assault 1, Torrent |
| **Grot Bomm** | 36" | 8 | 2 | Assault 1, One Use, Large Blast, Barrage |

**Supa-Skorcha:** A vehicle-mounted super-heavy flamer using a pressurised fuel mix that burns far hotter than a standard Skorcha. The ***Torrent*** rule allows the template to be placed up to 12" away from the vehicle rather than in base contact, representing the weapon's extreme range and fuel pressure. S6 AP3 makes this a terrifying anti-infantry weapon capable of melting through power armour.

**Grot Bomm:** A remote-controlled flying bomb "piloted" by a Gretchin strapped into the nose cone. The Grot steers the bomb toward the target via a crude control panel — assuming the signal doesn't cut out, the Grot doesn't panic, or the bomb doesn't just explode on the launch rail. ***Barrage*** represents the indirect flight path of the guided munition. ***One Use*** because the Grot (and the bomb) are expended on detonation. A vehicle may carry up to two Grot Bomms, each fired separately. **Grot Bomms are fired at BS3** (the Grot is actually steering the bomb, and Gretchin are better shots than Orks) regardless of the firing vehicle's BS.

### Big Mek — Designer Notes

> A Big Mek is a senior Ork Mekboy — a genius (by Ork standards) engineer, inventor, and builder of the weird and wonderful war machines that keep a Waaagh! rolling. He is not primarily a fighter like a Warboss; his value lies in his technical expertise, his Kustom Force Field, and his ability to keep vehicles running under fire.
>
> **Statline rationale.** WS4 and S4 reflect Nob-level physicality — he's still a large Ork and can hold his own in a scrap. BS3 is the key differentiator — the Big Mek actually understands ballistics and targeting, making him one of the most accurate Ork characters. W3 places him as a proper HQ-tier character without reaching Warboss durability. Ld7 gives him authority over the boyz without overshadowing a Warboss's natural leadership.
>
> **Role on the table.** The Big Mek serves two primary battlefield roles, defined by his wargear choice: a defensive support character with a Kustom Force Field (projecting a 5++ invulnerable bubble for nearby friendlies), or an offensive wildcard with a Shokk Attack Gun (unreliable but devastating). His Big Mek's Tools also make him the best vehicle mechanic in the army — essential for keeping Battlewagons and Junkas in the fight.
>
> **Mega Armour option.** Taking Mega Armour transforms the Big Mek into a slow, durable anchor. He loses mobility options (no Warbike, no Rokkit Pack) but gains the 2+ save, 5++ invulnerable, and access to the Tellyport Blasta. This represents the Big Mek climbing inside one of his own creations — a walking fortress of scrap metal and force fields.
>
> **Electric Armour.** This is a high-risk, high-reward piece of Mek technology that punishes anyone standing too close — including your own boyz. It creates interesting tactical decisions about unit placement and combat positioning. Do you embed the Big Mek in a unit for protection, knowing he might fry his own boyz? Or do you run him solo to maximize the Electric Armour's value?

### Mekboy Junka — Designer Notes

> The Mekboy Junka is the Big Mek's personal ride — a unique, ramshackle vehicle built from looted chassis, salvaged parts, and the Mek's own "innovashuns." Every Junka is different, reflecting the builder's personality and technical obsessions. In game terms, it fills the gap between a Trukk (cheap, fast, fragile) and a Battlewagon (tough, capacious, expensive) while offering unique customization through 'Speshul Bitz.
>
> **Vehicle profile rationale.** BS2 is standard for Ork-crewed vehicles. FAV/SAV 11 reflects the looted Rhino chassis that forms the heart of most Junkas, while RAV 10 represents the thinner rear plating. Per the OmniHammer Structure Point formula (11+11+10 = 32, falling in the 31–34 bracket), the Junka has 1 Structure Point — the same as a Rhino, which makes sense given the shared chassis. The Ramshackle rule provides additional survivability through sheer jankiness.
>
> **Fast and Open-Topped.** "Like all Junkas, Mekboy Junkas are fast, very fast." The Fast vehicle type combined with the Turbo-Charger means the Junka can cross the board rapidly. Open-Topped allows passengers to fire, making it a mobile gun platform as well as a transport.
>
> **Turbo-Charger.** The signature rule of the Junka. The risk/reward of the Turbo-Charger is quintessentially Orky — most of the time it works brilliantly, but one time in six the engine explodes and you're going nowhere. The +6" bonus stacks with the Fast vehicle type and Red Paint Job for potentially enormous movement distances.
>
> **'Speshul Bitz.** This is what makes the Junka unique. The ability to mount a turret-mounted Shokk Attack Gun, a Kustom Force Field, or a Supa-Skorcha turns the Junka from a simple transport into a specialist platform. The trade-off is reduced transport capacity (from 10 to 6), creating a meaningful choice between carrying capacity and firepower/utility. A KFF Junka zooming around the battlefield projecting a force field bubble is a classic Big Mek tactic. A SAG Junka careening toward the enemy lines with a teleportation cannon blasting wildly is peak Ork madness.
>
> **Ramshackle.** A simple, flavorful rule that represents the Junka's junkyard construction. Sometimes shots just pass through parts of the hull that were already holes. The 6+ ignore on Glancing Hits is minor but can swing a game — very Orky.
>
> **Grabbin' Klaw.** A new front-mounted option alongside the existing Reinforced Ram, Deff Rolla, and Wreckin' Ball. The Grabbin' Klaw features a unique "Grab an' Drag" mechanic — after a Ram, the Junka can seize an enemy model and drag it along for a punishing S8 AP3 hit. Survivors end up in base contact with the vehicle, setting up an immediate melee engagement. Against vehicles, it's a straight S8 AP3 hit to side armour — the Klaw digs in and tears plating off. This gives the Grabbin' Klaw a distinct identity: where the Deff Rolla is about crushing everything in the path and the Ram is about raw impact, the Klaw is about singling out a target and making their day much worse.
>
> **Da Mek's Ride.** The SAG mishap rule transfers firer-targeting effects to the vehicle itself. A "Zoink" result teleports the entire Junka (and passengers) across the battlefield. A catastrophic result inflicts a Penetrating Hit rather than removing the firer — the vehicle takes the punishment. This creates spectacular narrative moments: the Junka teleporting behind enemy lines with a Deff Rolla on the front is exactly the kind of emergent madness OmniHammer embraces.
