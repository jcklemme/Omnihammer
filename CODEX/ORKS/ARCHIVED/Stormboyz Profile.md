Stormboyz have the following rules:

***Klan Kultur***
***Mob Rule***

***'Ere We Go!:*** Stormboyz units may re-roll one die when rolling for difficult terrain tests during a charge move.

***Rokkit Pack Mishap:*** Ork rokkit packs are crude, volatile, and held together with wire, hope, and a generous application of red paint. After this unit completes a **THRUST** move, roll a D6:

| D6 | Result |
| :---: | :---- |
| **1** | **BOOM!** The unit suffers D6 Strength 4 AP— hits, randomly allocated. Armour saves may be taken as normal. Something blew up — could be a fuel line, could be a whole pack, could just be a Stormboy who sneezed at the wrong time. |
| **2–5** | **Nuffin' Happened.** The packs hold together. Barely. Smoke trails and sparks, but nobody's dead yet. |
| **6** | **WAAAGH! TURBO!** The unit may immediately move an additional D6" in any direction, following normal Thrust movement rules (ignoring underlying terrain). The controlling player gains **+1 WAAAGH! Energy**. Sometimes the rokkit pack works *too* well. The boyz ain't complaining. |

### Stormboyz — Designer Notes

> **What Are Stormboyz?** Stormboyz are Ork youths who rebel against the anarchic disorder of Ork society by doing the most un-Orky thing imaginable: following orders. They form into militaristic squads, drill obsessively, march in formation, and actually practice their combat techniques. Other Orks find this behaviour deeply disturbing and vaguely offensive. Most Orks view Stormboyz the way a biker gang would view a teenager who joins the army cadets — with a mixture of confusion, contempt, and grudging acknowledgment that the little git can fight.
>
> Stormboyz strap crude rokkit packs to their backs and hurl themselves across the battlefield in screaming, smoke-trailing arcs. The packs are unreliable, the landings are rarely graceful, and the casualty rate in training alone would make an Imperial commissar weep. But when a mob of Stormboyz slams into an enemy line at full speed, choppas swinging, it tends to make an impression.
>
> **Why Jetpack Infantry, Not Jump Infantry?** Classic Stormboyz are associated with the Jump Infantry archetype — strap on a rokkit, fly across the table, crash into the enemy. But Jetpack Infantry is a better mechanical fit for what Stormboyz actually do. The **Thrust** move (6" reposition in the assault phase if unengaged and not charging) represents Stormboyz using short controlled burns to reposition after shooting or to jockey for a better charge angle next turn. It's tactical, it rewards planning, and it's exactly the kind of thing a squad of obsessively drilled Ork youths would do. ***Relentless*** means they can fire their sluggas at full BS after moving without restriction — Stormboyz practice their shooting drills on the fly, literally.
>
> The trade-off is raw speed. Jump Infantry moves 12" (6" base +6" modifier). Jetpack Infantry moves 6" ignoring terrain. Stormboyz are no faster in a straight line than regular Boyz — they're faster in *practice* because they ignore terrain entirely and can reposition with Thrust. Their advantage is manoeuvrability, not raw pace. This is a deliberate design choice: Stormboyz shouldn't just be "Boyz but faster." They should be "Boyz who can get where they need to be," and that's a more interesting unit.
>
> **Statline Rationale.** The base Stormboy statline is identical to an Ork Boy with two exceptions: Initiative 3 (up from 2) and Leadership 6 (up from 5). Both reflect the obsessive drilling and squad discipline that defines Stormboyz culture. They're not physically bigger or stronger than regular Boyz — they're just *faster on the draw* because they actually practice, and *harder to rattle* because they've drilled for this exact moment. These are small bumps that make Stormboyz feel meaningfully different from Boyz without overstating the gap — they're still Orks, not Space Marines.
>
> The Stormboy Nob follows the standard Nob stat upgrade pattern: WS4, S4, W2, A3, Ld7. His Initiative stays at 3 (matching the base Stormboy) and his Leadership is 7 (Nob standard, one step above the Stormboy base of 6).
>
> **Rokkit Pack Mishap.** The mishap triggers exclusively on Thrust moves. This is a deliberate, opt-in risk: the player chooses to use Thrust, knowing there's a 1-in-6 chance of D6 S4 AP— hits landing on the mob and a 1-in-6 chance of getting a bonus D6" of repositioning plus +1 WAAAGH! Energy. The middle four results are clean — nothing happens, you made your Thrust, carry on.
>
> Tying the mishap to Thrust rather than to every move keeps the bookkeeping light (one roll per Thrust, and Thrust is already a deliberate tactical choice), avoids punishing the unit for basic movement, and creates meaningful decisions: "Do I Thrust to get behind that ruin, knowing the packs might blow? Or do I stay put and charge next turn?" That's a good question to ask an Ork player. The D6 hits spread the damage across the mob rather than killing a single boy outright — it's a bruise, not a death sentence, and 'Eavy Armour can mitigate it.
>
> The WAAAGH! TURBO result on a 6 feeds the WAAAGH! Energy economy and gives Stormboyz a wild reposition that no other unit in the game can match. Sometimes the rokkit works too well and the boyz end up somewhere nobody expected — including the boyz themselves.
>
> **No Mob Frenzy.** Boyz gain +1 Attack at 20+ models from ***Mob Frenzy*** — the primal, unthinking aggression of a massive Ork mob feeding on its own noise and violence. Stormboyz don't fight that way. Their aggression is channelled, drilled, and disciplined (by Ork standards). A mob of 20 Stormboyz is dangerous because they hit together, on target, from the sky — not because they've worked themselves into a frenzy. Removing ***Mob Frenzy*** keeps Stormboyz mechanically distinct from Boyz and reinforces the lore: these aren't a green tide, they're a guided missile.
>
> **Wargear Philosophy.** Stormboyz are a focused assault unit, not a weapons platform. Their job is to close distance, hit hard, and tie up or destroy the target. The option list is deliberately restrained compared to Boyz: no special weapon slots per 10 models, no Shoota swap. They get Krak Stikkbombs (to threaten vehicles on the charge) and 'Eavy Armour (to survive the journey), and that's it. The Nob gets the standard Nob melee and kombi-weapon upgrades because the biggest Stormboy gets first pick of the good stuff — same as in any Boyz mob.
>
> **No Dedicated Transport.** Stormboyz are Jetpack Infantry. The entire point of the unit is that they don't need a ride — the rokkit pack IS the ride. Offering a Trukk or Battlewagon would undermine the unit's identity and create awkward interactions where players buy a transport, strip the rokkit packs' value, and use Stormboyz as slightly more expensive Boyz in a box. Don't. Let them fly.
