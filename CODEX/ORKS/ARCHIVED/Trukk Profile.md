## RAMSHACKLE (New Faction Rule)

*Add to Ork Faction Rules document under a new "Vehicle Special Rules" heading or similar.*

***Ramshackle:*** When a vehicle with this rule suffers a result on the Vehicle Damage Table, roll a D6. On a 6, the result is ignored entirely — the shot passes clean through the vehicle's haphazard construction without hitting anything important. This roll is made after any modifiers to the damage result have been applied, but before Structure Points are spent.

## UNIT PROFILES

### Trukk

*Dedicated Transport / Fast Attack*

|  | BS | FAV | SAV | RAV | SP | Type |
| :---- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---- |
| Trukk | 2 | 10 | 10 | 10 | 0 | Vehicle (Fast, Open-Topped, Transport) |

A Trukk is a single vehicle model. When taken as a **FAST ATTACK** choice, a unit consists of 1–3 Trukks.

Trukks have the following rules:

***Klan Kultur***

***Ramshackle***

***Reckless Driver:*** This vehicle may make **TANK SHOCK** and **RAM** attacks as if it were a Tank vehicle.

**Transport Capacity:** 12 models.

**Access Points:** Open-Topped — the entire hull counts as an access point.

> A Trukk is the workhorse of any Ork warband — a crude, fast, open-topped transport slapped together from stolen parts, scrap metal, and blind optimism. No two Trukks look the same, but they all share the same essential qualities: they are loud, fast, unreliable, and on fire more often than not.
>
> Trukks are built to get Boyz into the fight as fast as possible. They are not built to survive getting there. The armour plating — if it can be called that — is scrap metal bolted on by whichever Mek was closest and least drunk. Shots frequently pass clean through the vehicle without hitting anything structural, not because of clever engineering but because there is so little structure to hit.
>
> What Trukks lack in durability they make up for in reckless aggression. Ork drivers treat their vehicles as weapons in their own right, swerving into enemy infantry and ramming other vehicles with gleeful abandon. The arrival of a Trukk mob — Boyz hanging off every surface, screaming, shooting, and throwing Stikkbombs — is one of the most terrifying sights on any battlefield.

### Designer's Notes

> **Paper-thin but fast.** AV 10/10/10 with 0 Structure Points means the Trukk will die to almost any dedicated anti-tank fire. That's intentional — the Trukk's job is to deliver its cargo, not to survive the battle. The Fast vehicle type gives it up to 24" flat-out movement, making it one of the fastest ground transports in the game. With Red Paint Job, that becomes 26".
>
> **Ramshackle.** The 6+ shrug gives the Trukk a small but meaningful chance to survive a hit that would otherwise destroy it. It doesn't change the math dramatically — you'll still lose Trukks regularly — but it creates memorable moments when a Trukk shrugs off a lascannon shot because there was nothing inside worth hitting. Note that Ramshackle fires after the Open-Topped +1 modifier to the damage roll, so it can potentially ignore an Explodes! result that was bumped up from Wrecked by the open-topped penalty. That's a feature, not a bug.
>
> **Reckless Driver.** Ork Trukk drivers are maniacs who view their vehicle as a weapon. Granting Tank Shock and Ram to a Fast vehicle is unusual, but it fits the lore perfectly and creates interesting tactical choices — do you deliver your Boyz or use the Trukk itself as a ram? Note that with AV 10 and S4 base (for ramming), the Trukk won't be cracking open Land Raiders, but it can threaten other light vehicles and will happily drive through a line of Guardsmen.
>
> **Fast Attack squadrons.** Taking Trukks as a Fast Attack choice in squadrons of 1–3 lets players field empty Trukks as mobile cover, objective grabbers, or ramming platforms without burning a transport slot. Each Trukk in a Fast Attack squadron may take its own upgrades independently.
>
> **'Ard Case interaction.** Taking 'Ard Case removes Open-Topped, adds +1 RAV (bringing it to 11), and prevents passengers from shooting out. This changes the SP calculation to 10+10+11 = 31, granting 1 Structure Point. The Trukk becomes a slow, enclosed tin can — tougher but losing most of what makes it useful. It's a deliberate trade-off.
