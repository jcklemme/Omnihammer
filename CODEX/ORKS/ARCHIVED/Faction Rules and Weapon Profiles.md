## ORK SPECIAL RULES

### Mob Rule

Orks don't run because they're scared — they run because nobody's watchin'. Or sometimes they don't run at all. Sometimes they do something much, much worse.

**MOB SIZE AS LEADERSHIP:** An Ork unit may use the number of models currently in the unit as its **LEADERSHIP** value for **BREAK TESTS**, **CASUALTY TESTS**, **PINNING TESTS**, and **REGROUP TESTS**, up to a maximum of 10. The unit may always choose to use its base Leadership from its **DATASHEET** profile instead, if it is higher.

**DA MOB AIN'T DONE YET:** If an Ork unit fails a **BREAK TEST**, **CASUALTY TEST**, **PINNING TEST**, or **REGROUP TEST** even after applying Mob Rule, the unit does not fall back, become pinned, or remain broken as normal. Instead, roll on the **MOB RULE TABLE** below to determine what happens. The result replaces the normal consequences of the failed test.

**Da Mob Rule Table (D20)**

| D20 | Name | Result |
| :---: | :---- | :---- |
| 1 | **Every Ork for 'Imself!** | The mob scatters completely. The unit immediately falls back 2D6" as normal. Additionally, the unit may not use Mob Rule for the remainder of the game — they've properly lost their nerve. |
| 2 | **Trampled in da Rush** | The boyz panic and stampede over each other. The unit suffers D3 Strength 4 AP— hits, randomly allocated, with no cover saves allowed. The unit then becomes **PINNED**. |
| 3 | **Shootin' at Shadows!** | The boyz open fire wildly in all directions. The unit immediately fires all ranged weapons using snap shots at the nearest unit (friend or foe) within 18". If no unit is within 18", the shots are wasted. The unit then becomes **PINNED** and may not shoot until the end of its next player turn. |
| 4 | **Blame da Grot** | The Orks turn on the weakest members of the mob. Remove D3 models of the controlling player's choice from the unit (this does not trigger further morale tests). The unit does not fall back but becomes **PINNED**. |
| 5 | **Stampede!** | The boyz bolt in a random direction. Roll a scatter dice — the unit immediately moves 2D6" in the indicated direction (use the small arrow on a **HIT** result). The unit stops if it contacts impassable terrain. If the unit contacts a friendly unit, that unit suffers D3 Strength 3 AP— hits. If the unit contacts an enemy unit, both units suffer D3 Strength 3 AP— hits but do not count as engaged. The unit then becomes **PINNED**. |
| 6 | **'Oo's da Boss?** | Two Orks start scrapping over who's in charge. The unit suffers D3 Strength 4 AP— hits (randomly allocated) as the boyz lay into each other. The unit may not shoot or charge until the end of its next player turn while the argument is resolved. If the unit contains a **CHARACTER**, that character suffers one additional Strength 4 AP— hit as the boyz test his authority. |
| 7 | **Dat Way, Ya Gitz!** | The boyz run, but in the wrong direction. The unit moves 2D6" directly toward the nearest enemy unit. If this brings them into base-to-base contact with enemy models, they count as having charged (but gain no charge bonuses and do not gain bonus attacks for charging). If it does not reach an enemy unit, the unit becomes **PINNED** at the end of the move. |
| 8 | **Krump Da Runts!** | The boyz turn on each other in a brief, vicious brawl. Each model in the unit makes one melee attack against the unit itself at its base WS and Strength. Roll to hit and to wound as normal, applying saves. Remove casualties. The unit does not fall back but may not charge until the end of its next player turn. |
| 9 | **Da Mob Splitz!** | The unit fractures under pressure. The controlling player must immediately split the unit into two roughly equal halves (as close to even as possible, controlling player decides which models go where). Both halves must maintain coherency. Both units count as being at their new, reduced starting strength for all purposes going forward. Neither unit may charge until the end of its next player turn. If the unit contains 4 or fewer models, treat this as **Blame da Grot** (result 4) instead. |
| 10 | **Smash da Gubbinz!** | Frustrated Orks take their anger out on their own gear. The controlling player must choose one special weapon or heavy weapon in the unit (if any). That weapon is destroyed and may not be used for the remainder of the game (the model may still fight with other weapons). If the unit has no special or heavy weapons, the unit suffers 1 Strength 4 AP— hit instead. The unit becomes **PINNED**. |
| 11 | **Hit da Dirt!** | Survival instinct kicks in. The unit immediately becomes **PINNED**. |
| 12 | **Mork's Disapproval** | A strange feeling washes over the mob. The unit and all friendly Ork units within 6" must immediately take a **PINNING TEST** (the original unit automatically fails this test and becomes **PINNED**). |
| 13 | **Da Boss Sez Stay!** | The boyz mill about in confusion but hold their ground. The unit does not fall back or become pinned, but may not shoot or charge until the end of the current player turn. |
| 14 | **Shiny Fings!** | The boyz get distracted by the nearest piece of terrain or wreckage. The unit immediately moves D6" toward the nearest terrain feature (not occupied by enemy models), stopping if this would bring them within 1" of enemy models. The unit counts as having moved and may not shoot until the end of the current player turn. The unit does not fall back. |
| 15 | **Leg It… Dat Way!** | The boyz don't flee — they just go somewhere unhelpful. The unit moves D6" in a random direction (scatter dice). The unit does not fall back or become pinned but may not shoot until the end of the current player turn. |
| 16 | **Squig Loose!** | Something gets loose from someone's pack. Place a marker in base contact with the unit. The marker moves 2D6" in a random direction (determined by scatter dice) at the start of each subsequent Ork player turn. Any unit (friend or enemy) it contacts suffers D3 Strength 3 AP— hits. The squig is removed if any model directs a shooting or melee attack at it (no roll needed) or at the end of the game. The unit does not fall back but becomes **PINNED**. |
| 17 | **Suck It Up** | The boyz grumble and curse but hold their ground. The unit does not fall back or become pinned and may act normally. Nothing else happens. |
| 18 | **Ded 'Ard** | Orkish stubbornness kicks in. The unit counts as passing its failed test and gains ***Fearless*** until the end of its next player turn. |
| 19 | **Seein' Red** | The boyz go berserk. The unit counts as passing its failed test and gains ***Rage*** and ***Furious Charge*** until the end of its next player turn. However, if there is an enemy unit within charge range during the assault phase, the unit must attempt to charge it. |
| 20 | **WAAAGH!!!** | The failed test triggers a localized WAAAGH! The unit counts as passing its failed test and gains ***Fearless***, ***Rage***, ***Furious Charge***, and +1 Attack until the end of its next player turn. Additionally, all friendly Ork units within 12" immediately pass any pending break, casualty, or pinning tests and gain +1 Attack until the end of the current player turn. The controlling player gains +3 **WAAAGH! ENERGY**. |

### Klan Kultur

Each Ork Klan has a distinct kultur that shapes how its boyz fight, what they value, and what they're rubbish at. When assembling your army, you must choose one of the Klanz listed in the **KLANZ OF DA ORKS** section. Each **DETACHMENT** in your army may only include Orks from a single Klan. An Ork model with the **Klan Kultur** special rule gains the benefits and drawbacks conferred by the detachment's chosen Klan.

### WAAAGH! Energy

The collective psychic fury and excitement of an Ork horde is a palpable force — a rising tide of aggression that feeds on violence and builds toward something unstoppable. In OmniHammer, this is represented by **WAAAGH! ENERGY**, an army-wide resource that the Ork player accumulates through acts of violence and spends to unleash powerful effects.

#### Generating WAAAGH! Energy

The Ork player tracks their current WAAAGH! Energy on a counter or die visible to both players. WAAAGH! Energy is generated as follows:

| Trigger | Energy Gained |
| :---- | :---: |
| An Ork unit successfully completes a charge move against an enemy unit | +1 |
| An enemy unit is destroyed (reduced to 0 models or removed from play) by Ork attacks | +D3 |
| An enemy **HQ** unit is destroyed | +3 |
| The enemy **WARLORD** is slain | +5 |

**Rules for Generating Energy:**
- WAAAGH! Energy is generated immediately when the triggering event occurs.
- A single event may trigger multiple sources of energy generation. If an Ork unit charges and subsequently destroys an enemy unit in the same assault phase, both triggers apply — the charge generates +1, and the destruction generates +D3 (or +3/+5 if the destroyed unit was an HQ/Warlord).
- The Ork player's WAAAGH! Energy has no maximum cap.
- WAAAGH! Energy persists between player turns and battle rounds and is never lost unless spent.
- Result 20 on the **MOB RULE TABLE** also generates WAAAGH! Energy.

#### Spending WAAAGH! Energy

WAAAGH! Energy may be spent **during any phase of the game** to activate effects from the **WAAAGH! ENERGY MENU**. The Ork player declares they are spending WAAAGH! Energy, names the effect, pays the cost, and resolves it immediately. Unless otherwise stated, each effect targets a single friendly Ork unit within 12" of the **WARLORD** or within 6" of any Ork **CHARACTER** model.

**The same effect may not be applied to the same unit more than once per player turn.** Different effects may be stacked on the same unit.

**WAAAGH! ENERGY MENU**

| Cost | Name | Phase | Effect |

### Warboss Ambition

Every Warboss worth his teef has a plan. It might not be a *good* plan — but every Ork in the warband knows what the Boss wants, and they fight harder when they're getting it done.

When assembling your army, choose one **WARBOSS AMBITION** for your **WARLORD**. The Ambition defines a condition that, when met during the game, provides an ongoing bonus to the army. The bonus persists as long as the condition remains true. **Warboss Ambitions have no effect if the Warlord is slain** — the boyz lose faith in the plan without the Boss around to enforce it.

| Ambition | Condition | Bonus |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Biggest an' Strongest** | Your **WARLORD** has more Wounds remaining than any single enemy **CHARACTER** on the table. | All friendly Ork units within 12" of the Warlord gain +1 Leadership. The Warlord gains +1 Attack. |
| **Krump Da Boss** | Your **WARLORD** is locked in close combat with an enemy **CHARACTER** or has personally destroyed an enemy **CHARACTER** this game. | The Warlord gains +1 WS and +1 Strength. All friendly Ork units within 12" gain ***Fearless*** while the Warlord is engaged in close combat with an enemy **CHARACTER**. |
| **Get Da Shiny Bitz** | Your army controls more **OBJECTIVES** than the enemy. | All friendly Ork **TROOPS** units gain ***Move Through Cover*** and +1" to their normal movement distance. The controlling player generates +1 WAAAGH! Energy at the start of each of their player turns. |
| **Stomp 'Em Flat** | More enemy units have been destroyed than friendly Ork units this game. | All friendly Ork units increase their charge range to 7" (instead of the standard 6"). The controlling player generates +1 WAAAGH! Energy each time an enemy unit is destroyed (in addition to the normal generation). |
| **Build Da Fort** | You control at least one **OBJECTIVE** in your own deployment zone AND at least one **OBJECTIVE** outside your deployment zone. | All friendly Ork units within 6" of an objective you control gain +1 to cover saves. All friendly Ork **VEHICLES** gain ***It Will Not Die (6+)***. |
| **Paint It Red** | At least 3 friendly Ork units have successfully completed a charge move this player turn or are currently locked in close combat. | All friendly Ork units gain +1 Initiative. Friendly Ork units that charged this player turn gain ***Hammer of Wrath (1)***. |
| **Da Great Loot** | Your army has destroyed at least one enemy **VEHICLE** or **MONSTROUS CREATURE** this game. | Ork **INFANTRY** models in the army gain +1 to their armor save (to a maximum of 3+) for the remainder of the game. Additionally, one Ork unit per player turn may re-roll failed armor saves of 1. |
| **Biggest WAAAGH! Eva** | The Ork player has accumulated 10 or more WAAAGH! Energy at any point during the game (whether or not it has been spent). | The Ork player gains an additional +1 WAAAGH! Energy from all generation triggers for the rest of the game. All friendly Ork units gain +1 to their WS for the rest of the game. This Ambition's bonus, once triggered, persists even if energy drops below 10. |

## KLANZ OF DA ORKS

When constructing your army list, you must choose one of the following Klanz. Each Klan confers a **Signature Rule** and a **Drawback** to all models with the **Klan Kultur** special rule. Models without **Klan Kultur** (such as Gretchin and certain mercenary units) are not affected by either the Signature Rule or the Drawback.

---

### Goffs — Da 'Ardest Boyz

*"We'z da biggest and da strongest! If you ain't fightin', you ain't nuffin'!"*

The Goffs are the most brutal and warlike of all the Ork Klanz. They hold close combat above all other pursuits and consider shooting to be vaguely unorky — unless it's at point blank range, right before a charge.

**Signature Rule — Born Scrappers:** Goffs models gain +1 WS when locked in close combat with a unit that has a higher WS than them. Additionally, Goffs units that successfully charge gain +1 Attack on the player turn they charge (this is cumulative with the normal +1 Attack charge bonus, for a total of +2 Attacks on the charge).

**Drawback — Shootin' is for Grots:** Goffs models suffer -1 BS (to a minimum of 1).

---

### Evil Sunz — Da Speed Freeks

*"RED ONES GO FASTA!"*

The Evil Sunz are obsessed with speed. They paint everything red in the unshakeable belief that it makes things go faster — and, terrifyingly, Ork belief being what it is, it might actually work.

**Signature Rule — Red Ones Go Fasta:** Evil Sunz **INFANTRY** models add +1" to their normal movement distance. Evil Sunz **VEHICLE** and **BIKE** models add +2" to all movement mode distances. Additionally, Evil Sunz **VEHICLE** models moving at flat out speed may fire snap shots during the shooting phase. **This is an exception to the normal vehicle rules, which prohibit shooting at flat out speed.**

**Drawback — Can't Sit Still:** Evil Sunz units that remained stationary during their movement phase suffer -1 to all To Hit rolls during the shooting phase and -1 WS until the start of their next player turn. They're too agitated to fight properly when standing still.

---

### Bad Moons — Da Richest Gitz

*"We got da biggest gunz, da flashiest bitz, and more teef dan you'll ever see!"*

Bad Moons are the wealthiest of Ork Klanz, blessed with the fastest-growing teef and an unquenchable appetite for ostentatious firepower. They festoon their equipment with gold plating, checkered patterns, and impractically large ammunition feeds.

**Signature Rule — Flashy Gitz:** All ranged weapons carried by Bad Moons models increase their maximum range by 6". Additionally, all Rapid Fire weapons carried by Bad Moons models gain +1 shot at half range (e.g., a Rapid Fire 1 weapon fires 3 shots at half range instead of the normal 2).

**Drawback — 'Eavy Pockets:** Bad Moons models are weighed down by all their flash gear. Their charge range is reduced to 5" (instead of the standard 6") and they may not use the ***Fleet*** special rule, even if it is granted by another effect (such as WAAAGH! Energy or the Mob Rule table).

---

### Deathskulls — Da Lucky Gitz

*"Iz not stealin', it's redistributin'! Gork sez it's mine now."*

Deathskulls are notorious thieves, looters, and scavengers who paint themselves and their gear blue for luck. They have an uncanny knack for finding exactly what they need among the wreckage of battle, and their superstitious rituals seem to pay off with disturbing regularity.

**Signature Rule — Lucky Blue Gitz:** Deathskulls models gain a **6+ invulnerable save**. Additionally, once per player turn, the Ork player may re-roll a single die roll made for or against a Deathskulls unit (this includes To Hit, To Wound, saving throws, morale tests, etc.). Declare the re-roll before any other dice are rolled.

**Drawback — Sticky Fingerz:** Deathskulls units that end their movement phase within 3" of a destroyed vehicle wreck, crashed flyer, or dead **MONSTROUS CREATURE** model must take a Leadership test. If failed, the unit spends the player turn looting and may not shoot or charge for the remainder of the player turn. They're too busy nicking bitz.

---

### Blood Axes — Da Sneaky Gitz

*"Tactics iz not un-orky! It's just krumpin' smarter!"*

Blood Axes are considered deeply suspect by other Orks for their willingness to engage in "un-orky" behavior such as retreating, using camouflage, and even negotiating. They are the closest thing Orks have to professional soldiers, which isn't saying much.

**Signature Rule — Taktikal Jeenyus:** Blood Axes units gain ***Scouts*** and ***Move Through Cover***. Additionally, Blood Axes models may fire Assault and Rapid Fire weapons at their full Ballistic Skill after making an advance move (instead of the normal restriction of snap fire for Assault weapons and no shooting for Rapid Fire weapons). Heavy weapons may not be fired after advancing, even by Blood Axes. **This is an exception to the normal advance move rules.**

**Drawback — Too Clever by 'Alf:** Blood Axes are too rigid in their planning. During the **DECLARE CHARGES** step of the assault phase, the Blood Axes player must declare ALL charges for all Blood Axes units before any charge moves are resolved. No Blood Axes charge declarations may be made or modified after the first Blood Axes charge move begins. 

---

### Snakebites — Da Old Ways

*"We don't need no stinkin' machines! Da old ways is da best ways!"*

Snakebites are traditionalists who reject much of Ork "technology" in favor of beast-riding, squig-herding, and good old-fashioned Orky toughness. They're looked down upon by other Klanz as primitive, but Snakebites don't care — they're tough as nails and their squigs are hungrier than your trukk.

**Signature Rule — 'Ard as Nails:** Snakebites **INFANTRY** and **CAVALRY** models gain +1 Toughness. Additionally, Snakebites models gain ***Feel No Pain (6+)***. Models that already have ***Feel No Pain*** improve it by 1 (to a maximum of 4+).

**Drawback — No Trust in Mekwork:** Snakebites **VEHICLE** models (except Walkers and Chariots) suffer -1 BS. Snakebites may not include more **VEHICLE** units (excluding Walkers and Chariots) than they have **INFANTRY** or **CAVALRY** units in the same detachment.

---

### Freebooterz — Da Flashest Pirates

*"We fight for whoever's got da most teef! And if dey ain't got enough teef, we take 'em anyway!"*

Freebooterz are Ork mercenaries, pirates, and adventurers who owe allegiance to no Klan. Their warbands are a motley collection of outcasts, fortune-seekers, and Orks who simply enjoy the pirate aesthetic. They fight for pay, plunder, and the sheer thrill of it.

**Signature Rule — Showoffz:** Whenever a Freebooterz unit destroys an enemy unit, all other friendly Freebooterz units that can draw line of sight to the destroyed unit gain +1 BS and +1 WS until the end of the current phase. This effect is cumulative if multiple enemy units are destroyed in the same phase (to a maximum of +3 BS/WS). The boyz get fired up watching their mates krump the enemy.

**Drawback — Pay Day or No Way:** At the start of each of the Ork player's player turns (beginning with the second battle round), if no enemy units were destroyed during the previous battle round, all Freebooterz units must take a **CASUALTY TEST** with a -2 Leadership modifier. If the battle isn't profitable, the boyz start thinking about leaving.

### Kombi-Weapons

All Kombi-Weapons consist of a **Shoota** (18", S4, AP6, Assault 3) combined with one or more secondary weapons. The model fires the Shoota and one secondary weapon each player turn, unless noted otherwise. The model must fire both at the same declared target.

| Weapon | Secondary Profile | Type |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Kombi-Skorcha** | Template, S4, AP5 | Assault 1, Gets Hot |
| **Kombi-Rokkit** | 24", S8, AP3 | Assault 1, Gets Hot, Backblast |
| **Kombi-Kustom Mega-Blasta** | 18", S8, AP1 | Heavy 1, Gets Hot |
| **Kombi-Skorcha-Rokkit** | See special rules below | Heavy, See special rules below |

**Kombi-Skorcha:** The Shoota's secondary flamer attachment may be fired every turn but risks overheating.

**Kombi-Rokkit:** The Shoota's secondary rokkit attachment may be fired every turn but risks overheating and produces dangerous backblast. When the Rokkit is fired, place a template with the small end in base contact with the rear of the firing model and the large end pointing 180° from the target. Any models fully under the template suffer a S3 AP— hit.

**Kombi-Kustom Mega-Blasta:** A Mek-only weapon combining a Shoota with a miniaturized energy projector. The energy weapon half fires at full Kustom Mega-Blasta power but risks overheating.

**Kombi-Skorcha-Rokkit — Da Full Loadout:** This weapon fires ALL THREE components simultaneously — the Shoota, the Skorcha, and the Rokkit — every time it is used. The entire weapon has ***Gets Hot*** and ***Backblast***. Additionally, on any To Hit roll of 1 with any component, roll a D6: on a 1–2, the wrong secondary weapon fires instead (if the Rokkit rolled the 1, the Skorcha fires at the Rokkit's declared target instead, and vice versa). Resolve the misfired weapon's hits against the original target using the wrong weapon's profile.

### PROPOSED NEW WEAPON SPECIAL RULES

> **Note:** The following weapon special rules are proposed additions to the OmniHammer core rules USR list. They are introduced here in the Ork Codex but are designed to be faction-agnostic and applicable to other codexes (e.g., Tyranids, Chaos Daemons, etc.).

#### ***Infestation***

A weapon with the ***Infestation*** special rule functions like ***Soul Blaze*** but represents a swarm of small creatures (squigs, Gretchin, borer beetles, Nurglings, etc.) infesting the target. When a unit suffers one or more unsaved wounds from a weapon with this rule, the unit becomes **INFESTED**. At the end of each subsequent player turn, roll a D6 for each infested unit:

- On a **4+**, the infestation persists. The unit suffers D3 Strength 2 AP— hits (no cover saves allowed) and must take a **PINNING TEST**.
- On a **1–3**, the infestation is cleared and the effect is removed.

An infested unit may only have one instance of ***Infestation*** active at a time. Additional hits from ***Infestation*** weapons do not stack but do reset the duration (roll again at end of turn as normal).

> **Future Cross-Faction Applications:** Tyranid fleshborers and devourers, Nurgling-based weapons, Chaos daemon swarm attacks, and similar creature-delivery weapon systems.

#### ***Warp Displacement***

A weapon with the ***Warp Displacement*** special rule causes hit models to be briefly shunted through warp-space and rematerialized nearby. After resolving wounds from an attack with this rule, each model that suffered an unsaved wound is moved D3" in a random direction (determined by scatter dice). If the displaced model is moved into impassable terrain or into base contact with another model (friend or foe), the displaced model suffers an additional Strength 6 AP2 hit with no cover saves allowed. If the displacement moves the model out of unit coherency, the model must restore coherency at the start of its next movement phase.

> **Future Cross-Faction Applications:** Harlequin flip belt weapons, Necron dimensional displacement technology, Tzeentch warp-shunt effects.

**Warbike:** A crude but fast two-wheeled machine bristling with guns. Ork characters who take a Warbike become significantly more mobile and durable, at the cost of not being able to join infantry units.

**Rokkit Pack:** A crude jet engine strapped to an Ork's back. Unreliable, dangerous, and exhilarating. Primarily used by Stormboyz, but characters can also take one to keep up with jump infantry units.

**Tellyporta:** A Mek-built teleportation device that shunts the bearer and nearby boyz through the warp. The technology is crude and the targeting is imprecise — units arriving via Tellyporta scatter further than those using Imperial or Tau teleportation systems.

### Vehicle Upgrades

| Wargear | Effect |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Armour Plates** | Vehicle gains **+1** to its **FRONT** and **SIDE** armour values. |
| **Red Paint Job** | Vehicle gains **+2"** to all movement mode distances. |
| **Boarding Plank** | Vehicle counts as an **ASSAULT VEHICLE**. Additionally, when a unit disembarks from this vehicle, all enemy units within 2" of the vehicle must immediately take a **PINNING TEST**. |
| **Stikkbomb Chukka** | Vehicle gains an additional weapon: 12", S3, AP—, Assault D6, Blast. |
| **Grot Riggers** | Vehicle gains ***It Will Not Die (6+)***. |
| **Grot Oiler** | One Use. At the end of any movement phase, the controlling player may attempt to repair the vehicle. Roll a D6: on a **4+**, repair one **Immobilized**, **Weapon Destroyed**, or **Engine Damaged** result. |
| **Searchlight** | The vehicle may illuminate a target unit during the shooting phase, removing **NIGHT FIGHTING** benefits for all units shooting at that target until end of the player turn. |
| **Reinforced Ram** | When this vehicle makes a Ram attack, add **+2** to the Strength of the ram hit. |
| **'Ard Case** | The vehicle is no longer **OPEN-TOPPED**. Additionally, the vehicle gains **+1** to its **REAR** armour value. Passengers may not fire from the vehicle. |
| **Wreckin' Ball** | Vehicle gains the Wreckin' Ball melee weapon (see **Melee Weapons — Walker & Vehicle**). |
| **Deff Rolla** | Vehicle gains the Deff Rolla melee weapon (see **Melee Weapons — Walker & Vehicle**). |

**Armour Plates:** Extra layers of scrap metal, tank treads, and stolen hull sections bolted onto the vehicle. Crude but effective physical reinforcement.

**Red Paint Job:** Every Ork knows red ones go fasta. The vehicle is painted a vivid red, and the collective belief of the Ork crew genuinely makes it faster. This is not superstition — it is a documented psychic phenomenon.

**Boarding Plank:** A reinforced ramp that drops from the vehicle's side or rear, allowing Boyz to charge directly into combat as they disembark. The thunderous crash of the plank hitting the ground and the sight of screaming Orks pouring out is enough to make even hardened troops flinch.

**Stikkbomb Chukka:** A mechanical device bolted to the vehicle that automatically hurls Stikkbombs at nearby enemies. Essentially an automated grenade launcher fed by a hopper full of explosives.

**Grot Riggers:** A team of tiny Gretchin mechanics that live inside the vehicle's hull, constantly patching leaks, tightening bolts, and making repairs during battle. They are never seen and probably don't want to be.

**Grot Oiler:** A single Gretchin with a can of oil, a spanner, and a very short life expectancy. Used for emergency repairs when the Grot Riggers aren't enough.

**Searchlight:** A powerful (if unreliable) lamp bolted to the vehicle's hull. Functions identically to an Imperial Searchlight.

**Reinforced Ram:** A heavy metal prow or set of tusks welded to the front of the vehicle, designed to increase the impact force when ramming enemy vehicles or fortifications.

**'Ard Case:** Armoured panels welded over the open top of a vehicle, converting it into a fully enclosed fighting compartment. The passengers lose their ability to fire from the vehicle but gain significantly more protection. The additional plating also reinforces the rear hull.

**Bosspole:** A unit that includes a model with a Bosspole may re-roll a single failed **BREAK TEST**, **CASUALTY TEST**, or **PINNING TEST** once per game. This re-roll is made before rolling on the **MOB RULE TABLE** (if applicable). If the re-roll passes, the unit does not need to roll on the Mob Rule Table.

## DESIGNER NOTES

> **Scope & Intent:** These faction rules are the first draft of the Ork Codex framework. Unit datasheets, wargear lists, psychic powers, and point values are not included and will be developed separately. The goal of this document is to establish the core mechanical identity of the Ork army — the rules that make them feel like Orks.
>
> **Balance Considerations:** The WAAAGH! Energy economy, Mob Rule table, Warboss Ambitions, and Klan rules are all subject to playtesting and iteration. Specific values (costs, bonuses, thresholds) are initial proposals and should be adjusted based on table experience.
>
> **Kitbashing & Modeling Notes:** The Klan system with signature rules and drawbacks is designed to encourage distinct visual identities for each Klan on the tabletop. Players are strongly encouraged to model their army to reflect their chosen Klan's kultur — Goffs should be covered in black and white checks, Evil Sunz should be red and fast-looking, Bad Moons should drip with gold and big guns, etc.
